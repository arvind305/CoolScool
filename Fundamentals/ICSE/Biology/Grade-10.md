# ICSE Grade 10 Biology Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Biology syllabus for Grade 10 is designed for the board examination level, providing comprehensive coverage of cell biology, genetics, evolution, plant and human physiology, reproduction, ecology, and environmental science. The curriculum emphasizes conceptual understanding, diagram-based learning, and application of biological principles to real-world scenarios.

---

## Theme 1: Cell Division
### Content

**Cell Cycle**
- Phases of cell cycle: G1, S, G2, and M phase
- Interphase: preparation for cell division
- Events during each phase
- Duration of cell cycle

**Mitosis**
- Definition and occurrence
- Phases: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, telophase
- Events in each phase
- Cytokinesis in plant and animal cells
- Significance of mitosis: growth, repair, asexual reproduction

**Meiosis**
- Definition and occurrence
- Meiosis I: reductional division
- Meiosis II: equational division
- Phases of meiosis I and II
- Crossing over and its significance
- Significance of meiosis: genetic variation, gamete formation

**Comparison of Mitosis and Meiosis**
- Location of occurrence
- Type of cells involved
- Number of divisions
- Chromosome number in daughter cells
- Genetic constitution of daughter cells

## Theme 2: Structure of Chromosomes and DNA
### Content

**Chromosomes**
- Structure: chromatid, centromere, telomere, chromatin
- Types based on centromere position: metacentric, submetacentric, acrocentric, telocentric
- Autosomes and sex chromosomes
- Karyotype: definition and significance
- Chromosome number in humans (46)

**DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)**
- Discovery of DNA structure (Watson and Crick)
- Double helix structure
- Components: sugar (deoxyribose), phosphate, nitrogenous bases
- Nitrogenous bases: purines (adenine, guanine) and pyrimidines (cytosine, thymine)
- Base pairing rules: A-T (2 hydrogen bonds), G-C (3 hydrogen bonds)
- Complementary strands

**Genes**
- Definition: functional unit of heredity
- Location on chromosomes
- Relationship between DNA, gene, and chromosome
- Alleles: alternate forms of genes

**DNA Replication**
- Definition and importance
- Semi-conservative nature
- Steps of replication (basic)
- Enzymes involved: DNA polymerase

**RNA (Ribonucleic Acid)**
- Differences between DNA and RNA
- Types of RNA: mRNA, tRNA, rRNA
- Functions of each type
- Central dogma: DNA to RNA to Protein

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 10 Biology, students should be able to:

- Explain the process and significance of cell division (mitosis and meiosis)
- Describe the structure of chromosomes and DNA
- Apply Mendel's laws to solve genetic problems
- Trace the stages of human evolution
- Explain mechanisms of water and mineral absorption in plants
- Describe transpiration and its significance
- Explain the light and dark reactions of photosynthesis
- Describe the structure and working of human circulatory system
- Explain the process of urine formation in kidneys
- Describe the structure and functions of human nervous system
- Explain hormonal coordination in humans
- Describe the human reproductive system and menstrual cycle
- Analyze population dynamics and biogeochemical cycles
- Evaluate causes and effects of pollution
- Propose conservation strategies for sustainable development

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 80 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 20 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Compulsory short answers covering entire syllabus
- Section B: Choice-based questions requiring detailed answers
- Diagrams are essential and carry significant marks
- Application-based and reasoning questions are emphasized

### Diagram Skills Required
- Cell division stages (mitosis and meiosis)
- DNA structure
- Monohybrid and dihybrid cross Punnett squares
- Root hair cell and root structure
- Stomatal apparatus
- Chloroplast structure
- Heart (external and internal)
- Nephron
- Neuron and reflex arc
- Brain structure
- Eye and ear structure
- Male and female reproductive systems
- Biogeochemical cycles

### Practical Activities
- Study of mitosis in onion root tip
- Identification of stages of cell division
- Experiments on osmosis and plasmolysis
- Transpiration experiments using potometer
- Photosynthesis experiments
- Study of prepared slides of tissues and organs
- Identification of blood cells
- Study of heart and kidney models
- Study of brain and eye models
- Field study of local ecosystem
