# ICSE Grade 6 Biology Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Biology syllabus for Grade 6 provides an introduction to the study of living organisms, their classification, structure, and functions. The curriculum builds a strong foundation in understanding the characteristics of life, basic cell structure, plant and animal diversity, human body systems, nutrition, health, and ecosystems. This foundational knowledge prepares students for more advanced biology concepts in higher classes.

---

## Theme 1: Living and Non-Living Things
### Content

**Characteristics of Living Things**
- Movement: ability to move or show movement
- Respiration: process of releasing energy from food
- Sensitivity: response to stimuli
- Growth: increase in size and complexity
- Reproduction: ability to produce offspring
- Excretion: removal of waste products
- Nutrition: obtaining and using food

**The Life Processes (MRS GREN)**
- Memory aid for characteristics of life
- All living things show these characteristics
- Examples from plants and animals

**Non-Living Things**
- Natural non-living things: rocks, water, air, sunlight
- Man-made non-living things: books, furniture, vehicles
- Characteristics: no growth, no respiration, no reproduction

**Differences Between Living and Non-Living Things**
- Comparison based on life processes
- Identifying living and non-living in the environment

**Once-Living Things**
- Definition: things that were once alive but are now dead
- Examples: dead leaves, wooden furniture, leather, paper
- Products from living things: wool, silk, cotton

## Theme 2: The Cell
### Content

**Discovery of the Cell**
- Robert Hooke and the cork cell (1665)
- Invention of the microscope
- Cell theory: all living things are made of cells

**Cell as the Basic Unit of Life**
- Definition of cell
- Cells are the building blocks of life
- Cells carry out life processes

**Parts of a Cell**

**Cell Membrane (Plasma Membrane)**
- Outer boundary of the cell
- Controls entry and exit of substances
- Present in all cells

**Cytoplasm**
- Jelly-like substance inside the cell
- Contains cell organelles
- Site of many chemical reactions

**Nucleus**
- Control center of the cell
- Contains genetic material (chromosomes)
- Controls cell activities and reproduction

**Cell Wall**
- Present only in plant cells
- Made of cellulose
- Provides rigidity and protection

**Vacuole**
- Storage sacs in cells
- Large central vacuole in plant cells
- Small vacuoles in animal cells

**Chloroplasts**
- Present only in green plant cells
- Contains chlorophyll (green pigment)
- Site of photosynthesis

**Plant Cell vs Animal Cell**
- Differences in structure
- Presence/absence of cell wall, chloroplasts
- Size of vacuoles

**Unicellular and Multicellular Organisms**
- Unicellular: made of single cell (Amoeba, Paramecium, bacteria)
- Multicellular: made of many cells (plants, animals)
- Division of labor in multicellular organisms

## Theme 3: Classification of Living Things
### Content

**Need for Classification**
- Easier study and identification
- Organization of knowledge
- Understanding relationships

**Basis of Classification**
- Structure and characteristics
- Mode of nutrition
- Cell structure

**Five Kingdom Classification (Whittaker)**

**Kingdom Monera**
- Prokaryotic organisms (no true nucleus)
- Examples: bacteria, blue-green algae (cyanobacteria)
- Some are useful, some cause diseases

**Kingdom Protista**
- Unicellular eukaryotes
- Examples: Amoeba, Paramecium, Euglena
- Found in water and moist places

**Kingdom Fungi**
- Non-green plants (no chlorophyll)
- Cannot make their own food
- Examples: mushrooms, yeast, bread mold
- Decomposers in nature

**Kingdom Plantae**
- Green plants with chlorophyll
- Make their own food (autotrophs)
- Examples: trees, herbs, shrubs, grasses

**Kingdom Animalia**
- Cannot make their own food (heterotrophs)
- Can move from place to place
- Examples: insects, fish, birds, mammals

## Theme 4: Plants: Types, Parts and Functions
### Content

**Types of Plants Based on Size**
- Herbs: small, soft stems (tulsi, mint)
- Shrubs: medium-sized, hard stems, branching near base (rose, hibiscus)
- Trees: tall, hard trunk, branching higher up (mango, neem)

**Types of Plants Based on Life Span**
- Annuals: complete life cycle in one year (wheat, rice)
- Biennials: complete life cycle in two years (carrot, radish)
- Perennials: live for many years (mango tree, banyan)

**Parts of a Plant**
- Root system: underground parts
- Shoot system: above-ground parts (stem, leaves, flowers, fruits)

**The Root**

**Types of Roots**
- Tap root: one main root with branches (dicots)
- Fibrous root: many similar-sized roots (monocots)
- Adventitious roots: roots from other parts

**Functions of Roots**
- Anchorage: holding plant in soil
- Absorption: taking in water and minerals
- Storage: storing food (carrot, radish)
- Conduction: transporting water upward

**The Stem**

**Types of Stems**
- Erect stems: grow upward (mango, sunflower)
- Climbing stems: need support (money plant, grapevine)
- Creeping stems: grow along ground (pumpkin, watermelon)

**Functions of Stems**
- Support: holding leaves and flowers
- Transport: moving water and food
- Storage: storing food and water (sugarcane, potato)
- Photosynthesis: green stems can make food

**The Leaf**

**Parts of a Leaf**
- Lamina (leaf blade): flat part
- Petiole: stalk attaching leaf to stem
- Midrib: main vein in the center
- Veins: network carrying water and food

**Functions of Leaves**
- Photosynthesis: making food using sunlight
- Transpiration: losing water as vapor
- Exchange of gases: oxygen and carbon dioxide
- Storage: in some plants

**The Flower**

**Parts of a Flower**
- Sepals: outer green parts, protect bud
- Petals: colorful parts, attract insects
- Stamens: male parts (anther + filament)
- Pistil/Carpel: female part (stigma + style + ovary)

**Functions of Flower Parts**
- Reproduction of plant
- Production of fruits and seeds

## Theme 5: Animals: Classification and Adaptation
### Content

**Vertebrates and Invertebrates**
- Vertebrates: animals with backbone (internal skeleton)
- Invertebrates: animals without backbone
- Most animals are invertebrates

**Classification of Vertebrates**

**Fish**
- Live in water
- Have fins and scales
- Breathe through gills
- Cold-blooded
- Lay eggs
- Examples: shark, salmon, goldfish

**Amphibians**
- Live on land and in water
- Moist skin without scales
- Cold-blooded
- Lay eggs in water
- Examples: frog, toad, salamander

**Reptiles**
- Have dry, scaly skin
- Cold-blooded
- Most lay eggs on land
- Examples: snake, lizard, crocodile, turtle

**Birds**
- Have feathers and wings
- Warm-blooded
- Lay eggs with hard shells
- Most can fly
- Examples: eagle, sparrow, penguin

**Mammals**
- Have hair or fur
- Warm-blooded
- Give birth to live young (most)
- Feed young with milk
- Examples: dog, cat, human, whale

**Classification of Invertebrates**
- Insects: six legs, three body parts (ant, butterfly)
- Spiders: eight legs (spider, scorpion)
- Worms: soft, long bodies (earthworm)
- Molluscs: soft body, often with shell (snail, octopus)
- Crustaceans: hard outer covering (crab, shrimp)

**Adaptation in Animals**
- Definition: features that help survival
- Physical adaptations: body structure
- Behavioral adaptations: actions and habits

**Adaptations in Different Habitats**

**Aquatic Adaptations**
- Streamlined body
- Fins for swimming
- Gills for breathing
- Webbed feet in some

**Terrestrial Adaptations**
- Legs for walking/running
- Lungs for breathing air
- Various body coverings

**Aerial Adaptations**
- Wings for flying
- Hollow bones (lighter)
- Feathers
- Strong breast muscles

**Desert Adaptations**
- Water conservation
- Thick skin
- Nocturnal behavior
- Examples: camel, desert fox

**Polar Adaptations**
- Thick fur or blubber
- White color for camouflage
- Examples: polar bear, penguin

## Theme 6: Human Body: Organ Systems Overview
### Content

**Organization of Human Body**
- Cells: basic unit
- Tissues: group of similar cells
- Organs: group of tissues working together
- Organ systems: group of organs working together
- Organism: complete living being

**Skeletal System**

**Functions**
- Support: framework for body
- Protection: protects vital organs
- Movement: works with muscles
- Blood cell production: in bone marrow

**Main Bones**
- Skull: protects brain
- Spine (vertebral column): protects spinal cord
- Ribs: protect heart and lungs
- Limb bones: arms and legs

**Types of Joints**
- Fixed joints: skull bones (no movement)
- Slightly movable: spine (limited movement)
- Freely movable: knee, elbow, shoulder

**Muscular System**

**Types of Muscles**
- Voluntary muscles: we can control (skeletal muscles)
- Involuntary muscles: work automatically (heart, intestines)
- Cardiac muscles: heart muscles (special involuntary)

**Functions**
- Movement of body parts
- Maintaining posture
- Producing heat

**Digestive System**

**Main Organs**
- Mouth: chewing and mixing with saliva
- Food pipe (esophagus): carrying food to stomach
- Stomach: churning and digesting food
- Small intestine: absorbing nutrients
- Large intestine: absorbing water
- Rectum and anus: removing waste

**Path of Food**
- Mouth to esophagus to stomach to intestines

**Respiratory System**

**Main Organs**
- Nose: filtering and warming air
- Windpipe (trachea): carrying air
- Lungs: gas exchange

**Breathing Process**
- Inhalation: taking in air (oxygen)
- Exhalation: breathing out air (carbon dioxide)

**Circulatory System Overview**

**Main Components**
- Heart: pumps blood
- Blood vessels: carry blood (arteries, veins, capillaries)
- Blood: transports substances

**Functions**
- Transporting oxygen and nutrients
- Removing waste products
- Fighting infections

## Theme 7: Food and Nutrition
### Content

**Components of Food**
- Six main components
- Each has specific functions
- All are needed for health

**Carbohydrates**
- Main source of energy
- Sources: rice, bread, potatoes, sugar
- Types: simple (sugars) and complex (starch)

**Proteins**
- Needed for growth and repair
- Building blocks of body
- Sources: milk, eggs, meat, pulses, beans

**Fats (Lipids)**
- Provide energy (more than carbohydrates)
- Store energy in body
- Sources: butter, oil, nuts, cheese
- Keep body warm (insulation)

**Vitamins**

**Vitamin A**
- For healthy eyes and skin
- Sources: carrots, eggs, milk, fish liver oil
- Deficiency: night blindness

**Vitamin B Complex**
- For energy release and nerve function
- Sources: whole grains, meat, eggs
- Deficiency: beriberi

**Vitamin C**
- For healthy gums and healing wounds
- Sources: citrus fruits, tomatoes, green vegetables
- Deficiency: scurvy

**Vitamin D**
- For strong bones and teeth
- Sources: sunlight, fish, eggs, milk
- Deficiency: rickets

**Minerals**

**Calcium**
- For strong bones and teeth
- Sources: milk, cheese, green vegetables
- Deficiency: weak bones

**Iron**
- For making blood (hemoglobin)
- Sources: meat, spinach, beans, liver
- Deficiency: anemia

**Iodine**
- For proper thyroid function
- Sources: iodized salt, seafood
- Deficiency: goiter

**Water**
- Essential for all body functions
- Transport of nutrients
- Regulation of body temperature
- Removal of waste

**Roughage (Fiber)**
- Helps in digestion
- Prevents constipation
- Sources: vegetables, fruits, whole grains

**Balanced Diet**
- Contains all nutrients in right amounts
- Varies with age, activity, health
- Importance for growth and health

**Malnutrition**
- Undernutrition: not enough food/nutrients
- Overnutrition: too much food
- Deficiency diseases

## Theme 8: Health and Hygiene
### Content

**Understanding Health**
- Definition: complete physical, mental, and social well-being
- Not just absence of disease
- Factors affecting health

**Hygiene**
- Definition: practices that maintain health
- Prevents diseases
- Types: personal and community

**Personal Hygiene**

**Bathing and Skin Care**
- Daily bathing to remove dirt and germs
- Clean clothes

**Dental Care**
- Brushing teeth twice daily
- Regular dental check-ups
- Avoiding excessive sweets

**Hand Washing**
- Before eating and after using toilet
- Proper technique
- Importance in preventing diseases

**Nail and Hair Care**
- Keeping nails trimmed and clean
- Regular hair washing

**Diseases and Their Causes**
- Disease: any condition affecting normal body function
- Germs/pathogens: microorganisms that cause disease
- Types of germs: bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa

**Types of Diseases**

**Communicable Diseases**
- Can spread from person to person
- Caused by germs
- Examples: cold, flu, chickenpox

**Non-Communicable Diseases**
- Cannot spread from person to person
- Examples: diabetes, heart disease

**Spread of Communicable Diseases**
- Air (droplets): cold, flu, tuberculosis
- Water: cholera, typhoid
- Food: food poisoning
- Direct contact: skin infections
- Insects (vectors): malaria (mosquito), plague (flea)

**Common Diseases**
- Common cold: virus, spreads through air
- Influenza (flu): virus, more severe than cold
- Typhoid: bacteria, spread through water/food
- Cholera: bacteria, spread through water

**Prevention of Diseases**
- Cleanliness and hygiene
- Safe drinking water
- Proper food handling
- Vaccination and immunization
- Isolation of sick individuals

**Community Hygiene**
- Proper sanitation
- Waste disposal
- Clean water supply
- Drainage systems
- Clean public spaces

## Theme 9: Habitats and Ecosystems
### Content

**Understanding Habitat**
- Definition: natural home of an organism
- Provides food, shelter, and conditions for survival
- Different organisms live in different habitats

**Types of Habitats**

**Terrestrial Habitats**
- Forests: home to many plants and animals
- Deserts: hot and dry, less vegetation
- Grasslands: grasses, grazing animals
- Mountains: cold, hardy plants and animals

**Aquatic Habitats**
- Freshwater: ponds, lakes, rivers
- Marine: oceans and seas
- Organisms adapted to water

**Understanding Ecosystem**
- Definition: community of living things interacting with their environment
- Includes living and non-living components
- Can be small (pond) or large (forest)

**Components of Ecosystem**

**Biotic Components (Living)**
- Producers: green plants (make food)
- Consumers: animals (eat food)
  - Herbivores: eat plants
  - Carnivores: eat animals
  - Omnivores: eat both
- Decomposers: break down dead matter (fungi, bacteria)

**Abiotic Components (Non-Living)**
- Air
- Water
- Soil
- Sunlight
- Temperature

**Food Chain**
- Transfer of energy from one organism to another
- Starts with producers
- Arrows show direction of energy flow
- Example: Grass -> Grasshopper -> Frog -> Snake -> Eagle

**Food Web**
- Interconnected food chains
- More realistic representation
- Shows multiple relationships

**Interdependence in Nature**
- All organisms depend on each other
- Plants need animals (pollination, seed dispersal)
- Animals need plants (food, shelter)
- Balance in nature

**Threats to Habitats**
- Deforestation: cutting down forests
- Pollution: air, water, land
- Hunting and poaching
- Climate change

**Conservation of Habitats**
- Protecting natural areas
- National parks and wildlife sanctuaries
- Reducing pollution
- Planting trees
- Individual actions

**Importance of Forests**
- Home to wildlife
- Produce oxygen
- Prevent soil erosion
- Regulate climate
- Provide resources

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 6 Biology, students should be able to:

- Distinguish between living and non-living things based on characteristics
- Describe the basic structure of a cell and differentiate plant and animal cells
- Classify organisms into five kingdoms
- Identify parts of plants and their functions
- Classify animals as vertebrates and invertebrates
- Explain basic adaptations in animals
- Name major organ systems and their functions
- Identify components of food and their importance
- Describe a balanced diet and deficiency diseases
- Explain the importance of health and hygiene
- Define habitat and ecosystem
- Construct simple food chains and food webs
- Appreciate the need for conservation

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 80 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 20 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Very Short Answer (MCQs and one-word answers) - 20 marks
- Section B: Short Answer (definitions, diagrams, differences) - 30 marks
- Section C: Long Answer (detailed explanations, flow charts) - 30 marks

### Practical Activities
- Observation of living and non-living things
- Study of plant and animal cells under microscope
- Collection and classification of leaves
- Study of different plant parts
- Observation of animal adaptations
- Testing food for nutrients
- Maintaining personal hygiene chart
- Study of a local ecosystem
