# ICSE Grade 8 Biology Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Biology syllabus for Grade 8 provides comprehensive coverage of life sciences, including plant and human physiology, reproduction, ecosystems, health, and food production. The curriculum emphasizes understanding of living systems through observation, experimentation, and real-world applications, preparing students for advanced biology studies.

---

## Theme 1: Transportation in Plants
### Content

**Transport System in Plants**
- Need for transport in plants
- Types of transport: short distance and long distance
- Tissues involved in transport

**Complex Permanent Tissues**

**Xylem**
- Structure: vessels, tracheids, xylem parenchyma, xylem fibres
- Function: transport of water and minerals
- Location in plant body
- Direction of transport: upward (unidirectional)

**Phloem**
- Structure: sieve tubes, companion cells, phloem parenchyma, phloem fibres
- Function: transport of food (translocation)
- Location in plant body
- Direction of transport: bidirectional

**Differences Between Xylem and Phloem**
- Structural differences
- Functional differences
- Direction of transport

**Absorption and Conduction**

**Diffusion**
- Definition and examples
- Importance in plants

**Osmosis**
- Definition: movement of water through semipermeable membrane
- Types: endosmosis and exosmosis
- Importance in water absorption
- Demonstration experiments

**Forces Contributing to Ascent of Sap**
- Root pressure
- Capillarity
- Cohesion of water molecules
- Transpiration pull

**Root Pressure and Guttation**
- Definition of root pressure
- Evidence of root pressure
- Guttation: definition and conditions

**Transpiration**
- Definition: loss of water as vapor through stomata
- Types: stomatal, cuticular, lenticular
- Factors affecting transpiration rate: temperature, humidity, wind, light
- Significance of transpiration
- Transpiration pull theory

## Theme 2: Reproduction in Plants
### Content

**Modes of Reproduction**
- Sexual reproduction

**Asexual Reproduction in Plants**
- Budding (e.g., yeast, Hydra)

- Spore formation (e.g., ferns, fungi)
- Vegetative propagation: natural and artificial

**Artificial Propagation**

**Sexual Reproduction in Plants**

- Structure of carpel: stigma, style, ovary
- Bisexual and unisexual flowers
- Pedicellate and sessile flowers

**Pollination**
- Definition: transfer of pollen to stigma
- Self-pollination: definition and examples
- Cross-pollination: definition and examples
- Agents of pollination: wind, water, insects, birds
- Features of wind-pollinated and insect-pollinated flowers

**Fertilisation**
- Definition: fusion of male and female gametes
- Process of fertilisation in flowering plants
- Double fertilisation
- Formation of seed and fruit

**Artificial Pollination**
- Definition and procedure
- Uses in agriculture and horticulture

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 8 Biology, students should be able to:

✓ Explain transport of water and food in plants
✓ Describe sexual and asexual reproduction in plants
✓ Understand human reproductive system and development
✓ Explain ecosystem structure and energy flow
✓ Describe functions of endocrine glands and hormones
✓ Explain structure and function of human circulatory system
✓ Describe human nervous system and reflex actions
✓ Identify common diseases and prevention methods
✓ Understand importance of hygiene and first aid
✓ Describe methods of food production
✓ Explain role of microorganisms in food and medicine
✓ Appreciate importance of conservation

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 80 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 20 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Very Short Answer (MCQs and one-word answers) - 20 marks
- Section B: Short Answer (definitions, diagrams, differences) - 30 marks
- Section C: Long Answer (detailed explanations, flow charts) - 30 marks

### Practical Activities
- Study of plant tissues under microscope
- Experiments on osmosis and transpiration
- Study of flower structure
- Observation of blood cells
- Study of heart and circulatory system models
- First aid demonstrations
- Study of microorganisms
