# ICSE Grade 8 Chemistry Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Chemistry syllabus for Grade 8 provides a comprehensive introduction to chemical sciences, covering the nature of matter, atomic structure, chemical reactions, and important compounds. The curriculum emphasizes understanding of fundamental chemical principles through laboratory experiments and practical applications, building a strong foundation for advanced chemistry studies.

---

## Theme 1: Matter
### Content

**Matter (Substance)**
- Definition: anything that has mass and occupies space
- Physical and chemical properties of matter
- Experiments demonstrating properties of matter

**Characteristics of Particles (Molecules) of Matter**
- Matter is made up of tiny particles (molecules)
- Molecules are in constant motion
- Molecules have spaces between them
- Molecules attract each other

**Kinetic Theory of Matter**
- Main postulates of kinetic molecular theory
- Explanation of states of matter using kinetic theory
- Inter-particle space and attraction in different states
- Effect of temperature on particle motion

**States of Matter**
- The Solid State: properties and molecular arrangement
- The Liquid State: properties and molecular arrangement
- The Gaseous State: properties and molecular arrangement
- Comparison of three states

**Change of State**
- Melting (Fusion): solid to liquid
- Freezing (Solidification): liquid to solid
- Evaporation: liquid to gas at any temperature
- Vaporisation (Boiling): liquid to gas at specific temperature
- Condensation (Liquefaction): gas to liquid
- Sublimation: solid to gas directly
- Desublimation (Deposition): gas to solid directly
- Energy changes during state changes

**Laws of Chemical Combination**
- Law of Conservation of Mass: statement and explanation
- Verification of law of conservation of mass
- Examples and applications

## Theme 2: Physical and Chemical Changes
### Content

**Types of Changes**
- Physical changes: definition and characteristics
- Chemical changes (reactions): definition and characteristics
- Differences between physical and chemical changes

**Classification of Changes**
- Slow and fast changes
- Natural and man-made changes
- Periodic and non-periodic changes
- Reversible and irreversible changes

**Indicators of Chemical Change**
- Change in color
- Change in temperature
- Evolution of gas
- Formation of precipitate
- Change in smell
- Change in state

**Conditions for Chemical Reactions**
- Contact between reactants
- Proper temperature
- Presence of catalyst
- Light (for some reactions)

## Theme 3: Elements, Compounds and Mixtures
### Content

**Classification of Matter**
- Pure substances vs impure substances
- Natural and synthetic substances

**Elements**
- Definition: simplest form of matter
- Classification: metals, non-metals, metalloids, noble gases
- Properties of metals
- Properties of non-metals
- Properties of metalloids
- Properties of noble (inert) gases
- Earth and its elemental composition

**Compounds**
- Definition: substances formed by chemical combination
- Properties of compounds
- Differences between elements and compounds
- Examples of common compounds

**Mixtures**
- Definition: physical combination of substances
- Types: homogeneous and heterogeneous mixtures
- Solutions, suspensions, and colloids
- Formation of mixtures
- Properties of mixtures

**Separation of Mixtures**
- Handpicking method
- Magnetic separation
- Gravitation (winnowing)
- Solvent extraction using separating funnel
- Crystallisation
- Sedimentation and decantation
- Filtration

- Centrifugation
- Fractional distillation
- Chromatography
- Diffusion
- Liquefaction of gases

## Theme 4: Atomic Structure
### Content

**Atoms: Building Blocks of Matter**
- Definition of atom
- Atoms are the smallest particles of an element
- Dalton's atomic theory: postulates and limitations

**History of Atomic Models**
- J.J. Thomson's atomic model (plum pudding model)
- Lord Rutherford's nuclear model
- Limitations of each model

**Discovery of Subatomic Particles**
- Cathode ray tube experiment
- Discovery of electrons (e⁻): properties
- Discovery of protons (p⁺): properties
- Discovery of neutrons (n): properties
- Nucleus: composition and properties

**Bohr's Atomic Model**
- Energy levels (shells): K, L, M, N
- Electrons revolve in fixed orbits
- Maximum electrons in each shell: 2n²
- Limitations of Bohr's model

**Structure of the Atom**
- Atomic number (Z): number of protons
- Mass number (A): protons + neutrons
- Number of neutrons: A - Z
- Electronic configuration of elements (up to 20)

**Isotopes**
- Definition: atoms with same atomic number but different mass numbers
- Examples: isotopes of hydrogen, carbon, chlorine
- Applications of isotopes

**Ions and Valency**
- Formation of ions: cations and anions
- Valency: combining capacity of an element
- Variable valency
- Types of ions (radicals)

## Theme 6: Chemical Reactions
### Content

**Types of Chemical Reactions**

**Combination Reaction**
- Two or more substances combine to form one product
- Examples: synthesis reactions

**Decomposition Reaction**
- One substance breaks down into simpler substances
- Types: thermal, electrolytic, photolytic decomposition

**Displacement Reaction (Single Displacement)**
- More reactive element displaces less reactive element
- Reactivity series of metals
- Examples and applications

**Double Displacement Reaction**
- Exchange of ions between two compounds
- Precipitation reactions
- Neutralization reactions

**Neutralization Reactions**
- Acid + Base → Salt + Water
- Applications in daily life
- Examples of neutralization

**Endothermic and Exothermic Reactions**
- Exothermic: release heat energy
- Endothermic: absorb heat energy
- Examples of each type

**Oxides**
- Definition and types
- Acidic, basic, amphoteric, and neutral oxides
- Examples and properties

## Theme 7: Hydrogen
### Content

**Introduction to Hydrogen**
- Position in periodic table
- Abundance in universe and on Earth
- Isotopes of hydrogen: protium, deuterium, tritium

**Preparation of Hydrogen**
- Laboratory preparation: acid + metal
- Apparatus and procedure
- Collection by downward displacement of water

**Manufacture of Hydrogen**
- Bosch process (water gas)
- Electrolysis of water
- Lane's process (steam over iron)

**Physical Properties of Hydrogen**
- Colorless, odorless, tasteless gas
- Lightest gas known
- Slightly soluble in water
- Neutral to litmus

**Chemical Properties of Hydrogen**
- Combustion: burns with pale blue flame
- Reaction with oxygen
- Reduction of metal oxides
- Reaction with chlorine

**Tests for Hydrogen**
- Burning splint test: burns with pop sound

**Uses of Hydrogen**
- As fuel (hydrogen fuel cells)
- Manufacture of ammonia
- Hydrogenation of oils
- Meteorological balloons

## Theme 8: Water
### Content

**Water: Our Lifeline**
- Importance of water
- Distribution of water on Earth
- Sources of water

**Water Cycle**
- Evaporation, condensation, precipitation
- Importance of water cycle

**Physical Properties of Water**
- Colorless, odorless, tasteless liquid
- Boiling point: 100°C at standard pressure
- Freezing point: 0°C
- Maximum density at 4°C

**Anomalous Expansion of Water**
- Water expands on freezing
- Significance: survival of aquatic life

**Water as a Solvent**
- Types of solutions: saturated, unsaturated, supersaturated
- Suspension and colloidal solutions
- Water as universal solvent

**Crystals and Crystallisation**
- Water of crystallisation
- Hydrated and anhydrous substances
- Examples: CuSO₄·5H₂O, Na₂CO₃·10H₂O

**Hygroscopic and Deliquescent Substances**
- Efflorescence: loss of water of crystallisation
- Hygroscopic substances: absorb moisture
- Deliquescent substances: absorb and dissolve

**Chemical Properties of Water**
- Reaction with metals
- Reaction with non-metals
- Reaction with metal oxides (forming bases)
- Reaction with non-metal oxides (forming acids)

**Hard and Soft Water**
- Definition and causes of hardness
- Temporary and permanent hardness
- Removal of hardness: boiling, chemical methods
- Advantages and disadvantages of hard water

**Water Pollution**
- Causes of water pollution
- Effects on health and environment
- Prevention and control measures
- Water management and conservation

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 8 Chemistry, students should be able to:

✓ Explain kinetic theory and states of matter
✓ Distinguish between physical and chemical changes
✓ Classify matter as elements, compounds, and mixtures
✓ Apply various separation techniques for mixtures
✓ Describe atomic structure and electronic configuration
✓ Write chemical symbols and formulas
✓ Balance chemical equations
✓ Identify types of chemical reactions
✓ Describe preparation and properties of hydrogen
✓ Explain properties and importance of water
✓ Describe allotropes of carbon
✓ Explain carbon dioxide and carbon monoxide
✓ Understand environmental issues: greenhouse effect, water pollution

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 80 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 20 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Very Short Answer (MCQs and one-word answers) - 20 marks
- Section B: Short Answer (definitions, equations, reasoning) - 30 marks
- Section C: Long Answer (detailed explanations, diagrams) - 30 marks

### Practical Activities
- Separation of mixtures by various methods
- Study of physical and chemical changes
- Preparation and testing of gases (H₂, CO₂)
- Study of water: testing hardness
- Crystallization experiments
- Testing for acids and bases
