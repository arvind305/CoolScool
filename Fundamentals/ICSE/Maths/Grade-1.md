# ICSE Grade 1 Mathematics Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Mathematics syllabus for Grade 1 is designed to develop foundational mathematical thinking and number sense in young learners. The curriculum emphasizes concrete learning experiences, manipulation of objects, and the development of fundamental concepts through play and practical activities. The focus is on building confidence and positive attitudes toward mathematics.

---

## Theme 1: Pre-Number Concepts
### Content

**Classification and Sorting**
- Classifying objects based on physical attributes
- Sorting by color, size, shape, and other observable properties
- Understanding categories and groups
- Grouping objects that belong together
- Identifying objects that do NOT belong to a group

**Seriation (Ordering)**
- Arranging objects in ascending order (small to large)
- Arranging objects in descending order (large to small)
- Understanding relative sizes and positions
- Creating sequences of increasing/decreasing size

**One-to-One Correspondence**
- Matching objects from one group to another
- Understanding "one-to-one" relationship
- Pairing objects correctly
- Developing counting readiness through matching activities

**Spatial Awareness**
- Understanding positions: above, below, in front, behind, left, right
- Understanding concepts: inside, outside, top, bottom
- Near and far relationships
- Positional language in everyday contexts

---

## Theme 2: Numbers
### Content

**Number Recognition and Numeration (1 to 20)**
- Reciting number names in sequence (1 to 20)
- Counting objects concretely (using actual objects)
- Counting pictorially (using pictures)
- Counting symbolically (using numerals)
- Recognition of numerals 1 to 20
- Writing numerals 1 to 20
- Understanding the concept of zero (0)
- Subitizing (recognizing small quantities without counting)

**Number Sense and Recognition (1 to 99)**
- Counting forward from any number up to 99
- Counting backward from 20 to 1
- Recognition of numerals from 1 to 99
- Writing numerals from 1 to 99
- Number names from 1 to 20
- Number names from 21 to 99 (gradual introduction)
- Understanding quantity representation
- One more and one less than a number

**Comparison and Ordering (1 to 20)**
- Comparing quantities using concrete objects
- Identifying which group has more, fewer, or equal
- Using symbols for comparison: >, <, =
- Ordering numbers from smallest to largest
- Ordering numbers from largest to smallest
- Arranging given numbers in sequence

**Place Value (Introduction for numbers 1 to 99)**
- Understanding tens and ones
- Representation of numbers using tens and ones
- Decomposition of two-digit numbers (e.g., 23 = 20 + 3)
- Building numbers using tens and ones blocks

---

## Theme 3: Number Operations
### Content

**Addition (within 20)**
- Understanding addition as combining groups
- Concrete representation of addition using objects
- Pictorial representation of addition
- Symbolic representation using the + and = signs
- Addition facts (0 + 0 to 10 + 10)
- Solving simple addition problems
- Word problems involving addition
- Commutative property: a + b = b + a (through activities)
- Understanding the concept of "putting together"

**Subtraction (within 20)**
- Understanding subtraction as taking away
- Understanding subtraction as finding the difference
- Concrete representation of subtraction using objects
- Pictorial representation of subtraction
- Symbolic representation using the - and = signs
- Subtraction facts (related to addition facts)
- Solving simple subtraction problems
- Word problems involving subtraction
- Understanding the inverse relationship between addition and subtraction

**Money (Introduction to Financial Literacy)**
- Recognition of coins and notes
- Understanding Indian currency: Rupees (₹) and Paise
- Identifying denominations: ₹1, ₹2, ₹5, ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100
- Counting coins and notes
- Simple transactions and exchanges
- Word problems involving money in real-life situations

---

## Theme 4: Geometry
### Content

**2D Shapes**
- Identification of basic shapes: circle, triangle, rectangle, square
- Naming of shapes in English and contextual awareness
- Drawing basic shapes
- Understanding properties of shapes (number of sides, corners)
- Sorting shapes based on attributes
- Comparing shapes
- Shapes in the environment (recognizing shapes in real objects)

**3D Shapes (Introduction)**
- Identification of basic 3D shapes: sphere, cube, cuboid, cone, cylinder
- Recognition of 3D shapes in the environment
- Properties of 3D shapes: rolling and sliding
- Understanding vertices, edges, and faces (informally)
- Comparing 3D and 2D shapes

**Patterns with Shapes**
- Identifying simple patterns in shapes
- Completing shape patterns
- Creating patterns with shapes
- Understanding repetition in patterns
- Extending given shape patterns

---

## Theme 5: Measurement
### Content

**Length and Distance (Informal)**
- Comparing lengths: longer, shorter, equal
- Using non-standard units for measurement (handspan, foot, stick)
- Understanding concepts: tall, short, long
- Arranging objects by length
- Simple estimations of length
- Introduction to standard units: cm, m (informal)

**Mass/Weight (Informal)**
- Comparing objects: heavier, lighter, equal weight
- Using simple balance scales
- Understanding concepts: heavy, light
- Sorting objects by weight

**Capacity/Volume (Informal)**
- Comparing capacity: more, less, equal
- Using containers for measurement
- Pouring and comparing liquids
- Understanding concepts: full, empty, half-full

**Time (Introduction)**
- Understanding time concepts: day, night
- Naming days of the week
- Months and seasons
- Understanding "before" and "after"
- Introduction to clock reading: hour hand and minute hand (simple)
- Reading the hour on analog clock (on the hour: 1 o'clock, 2 o'clock, etc.)
- Understanding daily routines and time of day activities

---

## Theme 6: Data Handling
### Content

**Collection and Organization of Data**
- Collecting simple data through observation
- Organizing data using tally marks (simple)
- Creating simple frequency tables
- Recording information in a structured way

**Representation of Data**
- Pictographs with one-to-one correspondence
- Picture representation of data
- Understanding how to read simple pictographs

**Interpretation of Data**
- Reading information from pictographs
- Answering simple questions based on data
- Making observations from represented data
- Understanding "most" and "least" from data

---

## Theme 7: Patterns
### Content

**Number Patterns**
- Identifying repeating patterns (1, 2, 1, 2, 1, 2...)
- Identifying growing patterns (1, 2, 3, 4...)
- Skip counting patterns (2, 4, 6, 8... and 5, 10, 15, 20...)
- Completing number patterns
- Finding missing numbers in sequences

**Shape and Color Patterns**
- Repeating patterns with shapes
- Repeating patterns with colors
- Extending given patterns
- Creating original patterns with given elements

**Patterns in Daily Life**
- Identifying patterns in nature
- Patterns in clothing and designs
- Patterns in classroom and school environment
- Cultural patterns and designs

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 1, students should be able to:

✓ Count and recognize numbers from 1 to 20 concretely, pictorially, and symbolically
✓ Recognize and write numerals from 1 to 99
✓ Understand one-to-one correspondence and classify objects
✓ Perform addition and subtraction of numbers within 20
✓ Solve simple word problems involving addition and subtraction
✓ Understand the concept of money and perform simple transactions
✓ Identify and name basic 2D shapes (circle, triangle, rectangle, square)
✓ Identify and recognize basic 3D shapes in the environment
✓ Measure length, mass, and capacity informally using non-standard units
✓ Read and understand simple time concepts
✓ Represent and read simple pictographs
✓ Identify and complete number and shape patterns
✓ Apply mathematical concepts in daily life situations

### Components
- Observation and continuous assessment
- Classwork and homework assignments
- Class tests and quizzes
- Project-based activities
- Group and individual activities
- Performance-based tasks

### Skills Evaluated
- Number sense and counting ability
- Problem-solving skills

- Ability to communicate mathematical ideas
- Application of learning in real-life contexts

## Important Notes

- The curriculum is designed for concrete, manipulative-based learning appropriate for Grade 1
- Emphasis on play-based and activity-based learning
- Speed is not the focus; conceptual understanding is paramount
- Individual pace of learning should be respected
- Language and mathematical vocabulary should be developed alongside concepts
- Integration of subjects (linking to language, EVS) is encouraged
- Field trips and real-world observations support learning
