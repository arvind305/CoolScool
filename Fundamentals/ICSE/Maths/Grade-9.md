# ICSE Grade 9 Mathematics Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Class 9 Mathematics syllabus forms the formal foundation for the Class 10 Board course and is structured into units: Pure Arithmetic, Commercial Mathematics, Algebra, Geometry, Constructions, Statistics, Mensuration, Trigonometry and Coordinate Geometry.

---

## Unit 1: Pure Arithmetic – Real Numbers and Surds
### Content

- Rational and irrational numbers; real number system.
- Decimal representation of rational numbers (terminating, non-terminating recurring).
- Concept of irrational numbers as non-terminating, non-recurring decimals.
- Surds: definition, simplest form and basic operations (add/subtract/multiply/divide).
- Rationalisation of denominators involving simple surds.

---

## Unit 2: Commercial Mathematics
### Content

**Profit, Loss and Discount**
- Successive discounts, overhead expenses, marked price, list price, net price.
- Profit, loss and discount in more involved multi-step problems.

**Compound Interest**

*Without formula:*
- Review of simple interest; amount and time-linked problems.
- Computing compound interest year-by-year (or period-by-period) by calculating interest on updated principal for each compounding period.
- Problems involving annual, half-yearly and quarterly compounding worked out step-by-step without a direct formula.

*With formula:*
- Derivation and use of the compound interest formula: $A = P\left(1 + \dfrac{r}{100}\right)^n$.
- Adaptations for half-yearly compounding ($r/2$, $2n$) and quarterly compounding ($r/4$, $4n$).
- Finding CI when $A$ is known: $\text{CI} = A - P$.
- Difference between simple and compound interest on same principal, rate and time.
- Inverse problems: finding principal, rate or time when other quantities are given.

---

## Unit 3: Algebra
### Content

**Expansions and Factorisation**
- Algebraic identities including:
  - $(a \pm b)^2,\; a^2 - b^2,\; (a \pm b)^3$ and related forms.
  - $(a + b + c)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + 2ab + 2bc + 2ca$.
- Expanding algebraic expressions using these identities.
- Factorisation by taking out common factors, grouping, using identities and special forms (difference of squares, sum/difference of cubes, trinomials).

**Changing the Subject of a Formula**
- Rearranging formulas to make a specified variable the subject.

**Linear Equations and Simultaneous Equations**
- Linear equations in one variable (consolidation) and in two variables.
- Solving simultaneous linear equations in two variables by elimination and substitution.
- Word problems leading to simultaneous equations.

**Graphical Solution of Simultaneous Linear Equations**
- Plotting two linear equations on the Cartesian plane.
- Identifying the point of intersection as the solution.
- Verifying the graphical solution algebraically.
- Understanding cases of no solution (parallel lines) and infinitely many solutions (coincident lines).

**Indices / Exponents and Logarithms**
- Laws of indices for rational exponents.
- Definition and basic laws of logarithms (base 10): $\log(ab) = \log a + \log b$, $\log(a/b) = \log a - \log b$, $\log a^n = n\log a$.
- Converting between exponent and logarithm forms.

## Unit 4: Geometry
### Content

**4.1 Triangles – Congruency**
- Congruence of triangles: definition and notation (correspondence of vertices).
- Criteria for congruence of triangles:
  - SSS (Side-Side-Side)
  - SAS (Side-Angle-Side)
  - ASA (Angle-Side-Angle)
  - AAS (Angle-Angle-Side)
  - RHS (Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side)
- Applications of congruence criteria to prove equality of sides, angles, or other geometric results.
- Medians, altitudes and angle bisectors of a triangle: definitions and basic properties.
  - A median connects a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side; the three medians are concurrent at the centroid.
  - An altitude is perpendicular from a vertex to the opposite side; the three altitudes are concurrent at the orthocentre.
  - An angle bisector divides the angle into two equal parts; the three internal angle bisectors are concurrent at the incentre.

**4.2 Isosceles Triangles**
- Theorem: If two sides of a triangle are equal, then the angles opposite to them are equal.
- Converse: If two angles of a triangle are equal, then the sides opposite to them are equal.
- Applications: proving results in figures containing isosceles triangles; using the theorems to find unknown angles and sides.

**4.3 Inequalities in Triangles**
- If two sides of a triangle are unequal, the angle opposite to the longer side is greater.
- Converse: If two angles of a triangle are unequal, the side opposite to the greater angle is longer.
- Triangle inequality theorem: The sum of any two sides of a triangle is greater than the third side.
- Exterior angle of a triangle is greater than either of the two interior opposite angles.

**4.4 Mid-point Theorem**
- Statement: The line segment joining the mid-points of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and is half of it.
- Proof concept using congruent triangles.
- Converse: The line drawn through the mid-point of one side of a triangle, parallel to another side, bisects the third side.
- Equal Intercept Theorem: If a transversal makes equal intercepts on three or more parallel lines, any other transversal cutting these parallel lines also makes equal intercepts.
- Applications to numerical and proof-based problems.

**4.5 Pythagoras Theorem**
- Statement: In a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides ($c^2 = a^2 + b^2$).
- Proof (using similar triangles or area method).
- Converse: If the square of one side equals the sum of the squares of the other two, the angle opposite the longest side is a right angle.
- Pythagorean triplets: common sets such as (3, 4, 5), (5, 12, 13), (8, 15, 17), (7, 24, 25) and their multiples.
- Applications: finding unknown sides in right triangles, distance problems, and combined figures.

**4.6 Similarity (Introduction)**
- Concept of similar figures and similar triangles.
- Corresponding angles are equal and corresponding sides are in proportion.
- AA (Angle-Angle) criterion for similarity of triangles.
- Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales' theorem): A line drawn parallel to one side of a triangle divides the other two sides proportionally.
- Applications: finding unknown sides using proportionality in similar triangles.

**4.7 Rectilinear Figures**
- Angle sum property of a polygon: sum of interior angles $= (n - 2) \times 180°$.
- Properties of a parallelogram:
  - Opposite sides are equal and parallel.
  - Opposite angles are equal.
  - Diagonals bisect each other.
- Special parallelograms:
  - Rectangle: all angles 90°; diagonals are equal.
  - Rhombus: all sides equal; diagonals bisect each other at right angles.
  - Square: all sides equal and all angles 90°; diagonals are equal and bisect each other at right angles.
- Trapezium: one pair of parallel sides; properties of isosceles trapezium (equal non-parallel sides, equal base angles, equal diagonals).
- Proving properties of quadrilaterals using triangle congruence and other results.

**4.8 Area Theorems**
- Figures (parallelograms, triangles) on the same base and between the same parallels:
  - Parallelograms on the same base and between the same parallels are equal in area.
  - A triangle and a parallelogram on the same base and between the same parallels: area of triangle = half the area of the parallelogram.
  - Triangles on the same base and between the same parallels are equal in area.
- Applications to numerical problems and proofs involving area relationships.

**4.9 Circle**
- Definitions: centre, radius, diameter, chord, arc (major/minor), semicircle, sector, segment.
- Chord properties:
  - A perpendicular from the centre to a chord bisects the chord.
  - Converse: The line joining the centre to the midpoint of a chord is perpendicular to the chord.
  - Equal chords are equidistant from the centre, and conversely.
- Arc and angle properties:
  - The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is twice the angle subtended at any point on the remaining part of the circle.
  - Angles in the same segment are equal.
  - Angle in a semicircle is a right angle (90°).
- Cyclic quadrilaterals (introductory):
  - Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary.
  - Exterior angle of a cyclic quadrilateral equals the interior opposite angle.
- Tangent properties (introductory):
  - A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.
  - Tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal in length.

## Unit 5: Constructions
### Content

- Construction of triangles given:
  - Three sides (SSS).
  - Two sides and the included angle (SAS).
  - Two angles and the included side (ASA).
  - Right angle, hypotenuse and one side (RHS).
- Construction of quadrilaterals (parallelograms, rectangles, rhombi, squares, trapeziums) given sufficient measurements.
- Construction of a regular hexagon inscribed in or circumscribing a circle.
- Bisecting a given angle using ruler and compass.
- Bisecting a given line segment (perpendicular bisector construction).
- Constructing angles of 60°, 90°, 120° and their bisected forms (30°, 45°) using ruler and compass only.

## Unit 6: Statistics
### Content

**Data Collection and Organisation**
- Collection of data; primary and secondary data.
- Frequency distribution tables for ungrouped and grouped data (class intervals, class marks, class boundaries).

**Graphical Representation**
- Bar graphs (including compound and component bar graphs).
- Histograms (including histograms with unequal class intervals).
- Frequency polygons (plotted from histograms or directly from frequency tables).

**Measures of Central Tendency**
- Mean:
  - Mean of ungrouped data (direct method).
  - Mean of grouped data using direct method and assumed mean (short-cut) method.
- Median:
  - Median of ungrouped data (arranging in order, middle value).
  - Median of grouped data using cumulative frequency and interpolation formula.
- Mode: mode of ungrouped data; identification of modal class in grouped data.

## Unit 7: Mensuration
### Content

**Area and Perimeter of Plane Figures**
- Circumference and area of a circle: $C = 2\pi r$, $A = \pi r^2$.
- Arc length: $l = \dfrac{\theta}{360°} \times 2\pi r$.
- Area of a sector: $A = \dfrac{\theta}{360°} \times \pi r^2$.
- Area of a segment = Area of sector $-$ Area of triangle (for minor segment).
- Area of triangles (including Heron's formula), trapeziums, and other composite plane figures.

**Volume and Surface Area of Solids**
- Cuboid: total surface area $= 2(lb + bh + lh)$; volume $= l \times b \times h$.
- Cube: total surface area $= 6a^2$; volume $= a^3$.
- Cylinder: curved surface area $= 2\pi rh$; total surface area $= 2\pi r(r + h)$; volume $= \pi r^2 h$.

**Application Problems**
- Cross-section problems: volume of a prism $=$ area of cross-section $\times$ length.
- Water flow problems: volume of water flowing through a pipe $=$ cross-sectional area $\times$ speed $\times$ time.
- Conversion between units of area and volume.
- Problems combining multiple shapes or solids (e.g., remaining area after cutting, melting and recasting).

## Unit 8: Trigonometry
### Content

**Trigonometric Ratios**
- Definition of trigonometric ratios for acute angles in a right triangle: $\sin\theta$, $\cos\theta$, $\tan\theta$, $\csc\theta$, $\sec\theta$, $\cot\theta$.
- Relationships: $\tan\theta = \dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$, $\cot\theta = \dfrac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}$, and reciprocal identities.

**Standard Angle Values**

| $\theta$ | $0°$ | $30°$ | $45°$ | $60°$ | $90°$ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $\sin\theta$ | $0$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $1$ |
| $\cos\theta$ | $1$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $0$ |
| $\tan\theta$ | $0$ | $\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$ | $1$ | $\sqrt{3}$ | undefined |

**Complementary Angles**
- Trigonometric ratios of complementary angles:
  - $\sin(90° - \theta) = \cos\theta$
  - $\cos(90° - \theta) = \sin\theta$
  - $\tan(90° - \theta) = \cot\theta$
  - $\cot(90° - \theta) = \tan\theta$
  - $\sec(90° - \theta) = \csc\theta$
  - $\csc(90° - \theta) = \sec\theta$
- Applications in simplifying expressions and solving equations involving complementary angle pairs.

**Trigonometric Identities**
- $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$
- $1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta$
- $1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$
- Simple proofs and applications of these identities.

**Heights and Distances**
- Angle of elevation and angle of depression.
- Solving right-triangle problems involving heights and distances using trigonometric ratios and standard angle values.

---

## Unit 9: Coordinate Geometry
### Content

- Cartesian plane, plotting points with real coordinates.
- Distance formula: $d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$.
- Section formula (internal division): coordinates of the point dividing the join of $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ in the ratio $m : n$ are $\left(\dfrac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m+n},\; \dfrac{my_2 + ny_1}{m+n}\right)$.
- Mid-point formula as a special case of the section formula (ratio $1:1$).
- Equation of a line: slope-intercept form $y = mx + c$; plotting straight lines from linear equations in two variables.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 9, students should be able to:

- Work confidently with real numbers, including surds, and rationalise expressions where required.
- Apply compound interest formulas (both step-by-step and formula-based) and solve profit-loss-discount problems in multi-step contexts.
- Manipulate algebraic expressions via expansion, factorisation, and change of subject, and solve linear and simultaneous equations both algebraically and graphically.
- Use logarithmic laws to convert between exponential and logarithmic forms and simplify expressions.
- Apply congruence and similarity criteria to prove geometric results about triangles and quadrilaterals.
- State and apply the mid-point theorem, Pythagoras theorem, and area theorems in proofs and numerical problems.
- Use circle theorems (chord properties, arc-angle relationships, cyclic quadrilateral properties) to solve geometric problems.
- Perform standard constructions (triangles, quadrilaterals, hexagons, bisectors) accurately with ruler and compass.
- Summarise data graphically and compute mean, median and mode for both ungrouped and grouped data.
- Solve mensuration problems involving circles, sectors, cuboids, cubes and cylinders, including cross-section and water flow problems.
- Apply trigonometric ratios, complementary angle relationships and identities in right-triangle contexts, including heights and distances.
- Use the distance formula, section formula and equation of a line in the coordinate plane.
