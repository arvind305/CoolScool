# ICSE Grade 10 Mathematics Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Mathematics syllabus for Grade 10 is the Board examination syllabus and builds on all concepts from Grade 9 in a structured way, covering Commercial Mathematics, Algebra, Geometry, Mensuration, Trigonometry, Coordinate Geometry, Statistics and Probability.

---

## Unit 1: Commercial Mathematics
### Content

**Compound Interest**
- Compound interest with varying rates and time periods (annual, half-yearly, quarterly).
- Growth and depreciation problems using the compound interest formula.
- Difference between simple and compound interest in applied contexts.

**Sales Tax and Value Added Tax (GST)**
- Computation of GST in buying and selling transactions.
- Input tax credit and net GST payable/receivable.

**Banking**
- Recurring deposit accounts: maturity value, interest earned using the formula $I = P \times \frac{n(n+1)}{2 \times 12} \times \frac{r}{100}$.
- Problems involving monthly instalments and total interest.

**Shares and Dividends**
- Nominal (face) value versus market value of a share.
- Dividend and rate of dividend: Dividend = Number of shares $\times$ Face value $\times$ Rate of dividend.
- Rate of return (yield) on investment: $\text{Return \%} = \frac{\text{Annual income}}{\text{Investment}} \times 100$.
- Brokerage and its effect on the cost of buying/selling shares.
- Problems involving choice of investment based on rate of return.

## Unit 2: Algebra
### Content

**Factor and Remainder Theorem**
- Remainder theorem: when a polynomial $f(x)$ is divided by $(x - a)$, the remainder is $f(a)$.
- Factor theorem: $(x - a)$ is a factor of $f(x)$ if and only if $f(a) = 0$.
- Applications to factorisation of cubic and higher-degree polynomials by trial of simple factors followed by division or synthetic method.

**Quadratic Equations**
- Standard form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ and solution by factorisation, completing the square, and the quadratic formula.
- Nature of roots using the discriminant $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$: real and equal, real and distinct, or not real.
- Word problems leading to quadratic equations.

**Ratio and Proportion**
- Componendo and dividendo: if $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, then $\frac{a+b}{a-b} = \frac{c+d}{c-d}$.
- Alternendo: $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \Rightarrow \frac{a}{c} = \frac{b}{d}$.
- Invertendo: $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \Rightarrow \frac{b}{a} = \frac{d}{c}$.
- Cross multiplication method for solving proportion equations.
- Applications to problem solving involving continued proportion and mean proportional.

**Matrices (2x2)**
- Order of a matrix; types of matrices: row matrix, column matrix, square matrix, null (zero) matrix, identity matrix.
- Equality of matrices.
- Addition and subtraction of matrices (conformable orders).
- Scalar multiplication of a matrix.
- Multiplication of two 2$\times$2 matrices; conditions for matrix multiplication.
- Solving simple matrix equations (finding unknown elements).

**Arithmetic Progression (AP)**
- General term: $a_n = a + (n-1)d$.
- Sum of the first $n$ terms: $S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n-1)d]$.
- Applications and word problems involving AP.

**Geometric Progression (GP)**
- Definition: a sequence where each term after the first is obtained by multiplying the preceding term by a fixed ratio $r$.
- General term: $a_n = a \cdot r^{n-1}$.
- Sum of the first $n$ terms: $S_n = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r - 1}$ when $r \neq 1$.
- Simple application problems involving GP.

**Surds and Rationalisation**
- Operations on surds (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) at Class 10 complexity.
- Rationalisation of binomial surd denominators.
- Simplification of expressions involving surds using algebraic identities.

## Unit 3: Geometry
### Content

**Similarity of Triangles**
- Criteria for similarity: AA, SAS, SSS.
- Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales' theorem) and its converse.
- Areas of similar triangles (ratio of areas equals square of ratio of corresponding sides).
- Applications and proof-based problems.

**Circles**
- Angle properties:
  - Angle in a semicircle is 90°.
  - Angles in the same segment are equal.
  - Angle at the centre is twice the angle at the circumference subtended by the same arc.
  - Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary.
- Tangent properties:
  - A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.
  - Tangents drawn from an external point are equal in length.
- Intersecting chords and secant-tangent relationships.

**Constructions**
- Construction of tangents to a circle from an external point.
- Construction of circumscribed and inscribed circles of a triangle.
- Constructions based on given conditions involving triangles and circles.

**Loci**
- Definition: a locus is the set of all points satisfying a given geometric condition.
- Standard locus theorems:
  - The locus of a point equidistant from two fixed points is the perpendicular bisector of the line segment joining them.
  - The locus of a point equidistant from two intersecting lines is the pair of angle bisectors.
  - The locus of a point at a given distance from a fixed point is a circle.
  - The locus of a point at a given distance from a fixed line is a pair of parallel lines.
- Intersection of two loci to locate points satisfying two conditions simultaneously.
- Construction problems involving loci.

## Unit 4: Mensuration
### Content

- Surface area and volume of solids: cylinder, cone, sphere, hemisphere.
- Combination of solids: problems involving solids formed by joining two or more standard shapes (e.g., a cone mounted on a cylinder, a hemisphere on a cone).
- Conversion problems: recasting one solid into another (e.g., melting a sphere to form small cylinders).
- Capacity and optimisation-type application problems.

---

## Unit 5: Trigonometry
### Content

**Trigonometric Identities**
- The three fundamental identities:
  - $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$
  - $1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta$
  - $1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$
- Proving identities and simplifying expressions using these relationships.

**Complementary Angles**
- $\sin(90° - \theta) = \cos\theta$, $\tan(90° - \theta) = \cot\theta$, etc.
- Application in simplification and evaluation.

**Heights and Distances**
- Angle of elevation and angle of depression.
- Problems involving one or two right triangles, including multi-step scenarios.

---

## Unit 6: Coordinate Geometry
### Content

**Equation of a Straight Line**
- Slope (gradient) of a line and y-intercept.
- Equation in slope-intercept form $y = mx + c$.
- Equation from two points; point-slope form $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$.
- Conditions for two lines to be parallel ($m_1 = m_2$) or perpendicular ($m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$).

**Distance and Section Formulae**
- Distance between two points: $d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$.
- Section formula for internal division: coordinates of the point dividing the join of $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ in the ratio $m:n$.
- Mid-point formula as a special case.

**Reflection**
- Reflection of a point in the x-axis: $(x, y) \to (x, -y)$.
- Reflection of a point in the y-axis: $(x, y) \to (-x, y)$.
- Reflection of a point in the origin: $(x, y) \to (-x, -y)$.
- Reflection of a point in the line $y = x$: $(x, y) \to (y, x)$.
- Using reflections to find images of points and deduce geometric properties.

---

## Unit 7: Statistics
### Content

- Frequency distributions for grouped data; class intervals, class marks, cumulative frequency.
- Graphical representation: histograms, frequency polygons, ogives (cumulative frequency curves).
- Measures of central tendency for grouped data:
  - Mean by direct, assumed-mean, and step-deviation methods.
  - Median from ogive (graphical method) and by formula.
  - Mode from histogram (graphical method) and by formula.
- Interpretation of statistical results in context.

---

## Unit 8: Probability
### Content

- Random experiments, outcomes, sample space.
- Events: definition, favourable outcomes.
- Probability of a single event: $P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of favourable outcomes}}{\text{Total number of outcomes}}$, where $0 \leq P(E) \leq 1$.
- Complementary events: $P(E) + P(\overline{E}) = 1$.
- Simple problems based on:
  - Tossing one or two coins.
  - Throwing one or two dice.
  - Drawing a card from a well-shuffled pack of 52 cards.
  - Drawing a ball from a bag containing balls of different colours.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 10, students should be able to:

- Demonstrate mastery of algebraic manipulation including surds, polynomials, factor/remainder theorem, ratio-proportion, matrices and quadratic equations at Board-exam level.
- Apply commercial mathematics to realistic financial situations involving banking, shares and dividends, GST and compound interest.
- Solve quadratic equations and apply AP/GP formulae in structured and word problems.
- Use formal geometric theorems (similarity, circle theorems, loci) to prove results and solve non-routine geometry problems.
- Handle mensuration of single and composite solids accurately and interpret results in real-world contexts.
- Prove and apply trigonometric identities and solve heights-and-distances problems involving right triangles.
- Represent relationships graphically in the coordinate plane, interpret slope and intercept, and apply reflections.
- Summarise, represent and interpret statistical data using appropriate tabular and graphical tools, and compute central tendency for grouped data.
- Calculate probability of simple events and apply the concept to problems on coins, dice and cards.

## Assessment

- **Board examination**: 80-mark theory paper set by CISCE with structured, short and long-answer questions.
- **Internal assessment**: 20 marks based on project work, assignments, and practical/graphical work evaluated by the school according to CISCE guidelines.
