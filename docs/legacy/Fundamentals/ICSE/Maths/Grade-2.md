# ICSE Grade 2 Mathematics Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Mathematics syllabus for Grade 2 builds upon the foundational concepts introduced in Grade 1, strengthening number sense and extending mathematical understanding to three-digit numbers. The curriculum emphasizes conceptual understanding through concrete, pictorial, and abstract representations, while introducing more complex operations and problem-solving skills. Students transition from manipulative-based learning to more symbolic mathematical thinking.

---

## Theme 1: Numbers
### Content

**Numbers up to 200**
- Reading and writing numbers from 100 to 200
- Number names from 100 to 200
- Counting forward and backward from any number up to 200
- Recognition and writing of numerals
- Understanding the concept of hundreds, tens, and ones
- Place value in three-digit numbers (up to 200)
- Expanded form and short form of numbers
- Comparison of numbers using >, <, = symbols
- Ordering numbers from smallest to largest and vice versa
- Number line representation up to 200

**Numbers up to 999**
- Reading and writing numbers from 201 to 999
- Number names from 201 to 999
- Counting in ones, tens, and hundreds up to 999
- Recognition and formation of three-digit numbers
- Understanding place value: hundreds, tens, ones
- Expanded form: e.g., 456 = 400 + 50 + 6
- Short form and standard form of numbers
- Face value and place value distinction
- Comparison and ordering of three-digit numbers
- Number patterns within 999
- Forming greatest and smallest numbers with given digits (with and without repetition)

**Number Line and Skip Counting**
- Representation of numbers on number line
- Skip counting by 2s, 3s, 4s, 5s, and 10s
- Understanding patterns in skip counting
- Forward and backward counting in multiples
- Number sequences and missing numbers

**Even and Odd Numbers**
- Identifying even and odd numbers up to 100
- Properties of even and odd numbers
- Patterns in even and odd numbers
- Understanding that even numbers are divisible by 2

## Theme 2: Number Operations
### Content

**Addition (up to 999)**
- Addition without regrouping (carrying)
- Addition with regrouping (carrying) for two-digit numbers
- Addition with regrouping for three-digit numbers *(stretch/enrichment only — standard scope is 3-digit without regrouping)*
- Column addition and horizontal addition
- Adding zero to any number (identity property)
- Commutative property of addition: a + b = b + a
- Associative property of addition (through activities)
- Estimation of sums
- Word problems involving addition
- Real-life application of addition (money, measurement)
- Mental addition strategies for two-digit numbers

**Subtraction (up to 999)**
- Subtraction without regrouping (borrowing)
- Subtraction with regrouping (borrowing) for two-digit numbers
- Subtraction with regrouping for three-digit numbers *(stretch/enrichment only — standard scope is 3-digit without regrouping)*
- Column subtraction and horizontal subtraction
- Subtracting zero from any number
- Understanding subtraction as inverse of addition
- Checking subtraction using addition
- Estimation of differences
- Word problems involving subtraction
- Real-life application of subtraction
- Mental subtraction strategies for two-digit numbers

**Mixed Operations**
- Problems involving both addition and subtraction
- Determining which operation to use in word problems
- Multi-step word problems (simple)
- Relationship between addition and subtraction

**Multiplication (Introduction and Tables)**
- Understanding multiplication as repeated addition
- Concept of equal groups
- Multiplication symbol: × and its meaning
- Multiplication tables: 2, 3, 4, 5, and 10
- Skip counting as multiplication
- Array representation of multiplication
- Commutative property of multiplication (through patterns)
- Word problems involving multiplication
- Relationship between multiplication and addition

**Division (Informal Introduction)**
- Understanding division as equal sharing through hands-on activities
- Understanding division as equal grouping using concrete objects
- Word problems involving equal sharing among small groups (2, 3, 4, 5)

**Money**
- Indian currency: rupees and paise
- Recognition of all denominations: ₹1, ₹2, ₹5, ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100, ₹500
- Converting rupees to paise and vice versa
- Addition and subtraction of money (up to ₹100)
- Making change for transactions
- Word problems involving money
- Real-life shopping scenarios
- Understanding bills and simple budgeting

## Theme 3: Geometry
### Content

**Basic Geometrical Concepts**
- Straight lines and curved lines
- Open and closed figures
- Drawing straight lines with and without ruler
- Free-hand drawing vs. ruler drawing

**2D Shapes (Plane Figures)**
- Review of basic shapes: circle, triangle, square, rectangle
- Introduction to new shapes: oval (ellipse), pentagon, hexagon
- Properties of shapes: number of sides, corners (vertices)
- Comparing and contrasting shapes
- Symmetry in shapes (informal introduction)
- Line of symmetry (simple examples)
- Shapes in the environment and daily life
- Creating patterns with shapes
- Tracing and drawing shapes

**3D Shapes (Solid Figures)**
- Review of 3D shapes: sphere, cube, cuboid, cone, cylinder
- Introduction to pyramid
- Properties of 3D shapes: faces, edges, vertices
- Distinguishing between shapes that roll and slide
- Relationship between 2D and 3D shapes (faces of 3D shapes)
- Real-life objects as 3D shapes
- Comparing 2D and 3D shapes

**Lines and Curves**
- Identifying straight and curved lines in objects
- Drawing curves and straight lines
- Closed and open curves
- Simple closed curves
- Understanding boundaries

---

## Theme 4: Measurement
### Content

**Length and Distance**
- Non-standard units: handspan, footstep, pencil length
- Standard units: centimeter (cm), meter (m)
- Using a ruler to measure length in cm
- Conversion: meters to centimeters (simple conversions)
- Measuring objects in the classroom and at home
- Estimating length before measuring
- Comparison of lengths: longer, shorter, equal, longest, shortest
- Word problems involving length
- Drawing line segments of given length

**Mass and Weight**
- Understanding the concept of mass/weight
- Non-standard units: stones, marbles
- Standard units: gram (g), kilogram (kg)
- Using weighing scales
- Conversion: kilograms to grams (simple conversions)
- Comparison: heavier, lighter, equal weight, heaviest, lightest
- Estimating weight of objects
- Word problems involving weight

**Capacity and Volume**
- Understanding capacity as amount a container holds
- Non-standard units: cups, glasses, spoons
- Standard units: milliliter (ml), liter (l)
- Using measuring containers
- Conversion: liters to milliliters (simple conversions)
- Comparison: more capacity, less capacity, equal capacity
- Estimating capacity
- Word problems involving capacity
- Full, half-full, empty concepts

**Time**
- Days of the week in sequence
- Months of the year in sequence
- Understanding calendar: date, day, month, year
- Seasons and their characteristics
- Reading time on analog clock: hours and half hours
- Reading time on digital clock: hours and minutes
- 12-hour format: AM and PM (introduction)
- Duration of time: hours and minutes
- Yesterday, today, tomorrow concepts
- Before and after in time context
- Simple time calculations
- Word problems involving time

## Theme 5: Data Handling
### Content

**Collection and Organization of Data**
- Collecting data through surveys and observations
- Recording data in simple tables
- Organizing information systematically
- Tally marks for counting and recording
- Frequency tables (simple)

**Representation of Data**
- Pictographs with one-to-one correspondence
- Pictographs with scale (one symbol = 2 or 5 units)
- Simple bar graphs (vertical and horizontal)
- Understanding axes and labels
- Choosing appropriate representation for data

**Interpretation of Data**
- Reading information from pictographs
- Reading information from bar graphs
- Reading information from tables
- Answering questions based on data
- Finding most and least from data
- Making simple comparisons from data
- Drawing simple conclusions

## Theme 6: Patterns
### Content

**Number Patterns**
- Identifying and extending number patterns
- Repeating patterns with numbers
- Growing patterns: ascending and descending
- Skip counting patterns (multiples)
- Finding missing numbers in patterns
- Creating number patterns with given rules
- Even and odd number patterns
- Arithmetic sequences (simple)

**Shape and Color Patterns**
- Repeating patterns with shapes and colors
- Alternating patterns
- Complex repeating patterns (ABC, AABB types)
- Extending shape patterns
- Creating original shape patterns
- Symmetrical patterns

**Patterns in Real Life**
- Identifying patterns in nature (flowers, leaves, shells)
- Patterns in architecture and design
- Patterns in clothing and textiles
- Cultural and traditional patterns
- Creating art using patterns

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 2, students should be able to:

✓ Read, write, and recognize numbers up to 999
✓ Understand place value of hundreds, tens, and ones
✓ Form greatest and smallest numbers with given digits
✓ Perform addition and subtraction with regrouping for two-digit numbers and without regrouping for three-digit numbers
✓ Solve word problems involving addition and subtraction
✓ Recall multiplication tables for 2, 3, 4, 5, and 10
✓ Understand multiplication as repeated addition and division as equal sharing
✓ Work with money: addition, subtraction, and making change
✓ Identify and describe properties of 2D and 3D shapes
✓ Measure length, mass, capacity, and time using standard units
✓ Read time on analog and digital clocks (hours and half hours)
✓ Collect, organize, and represent data using pictographs and bar graphs
✓ Interpret information from tables and graphs
✓ Identify, extend, and create various patterns
✓ Apply mathematical concepts to solve real-life problems
✓ Demonstrate mental math skills for simple calculations

## Assessment

### Components
- Continuous and comprehensive assessment
- Class tests and periodic assessments
- Oral tests for tables and mental math
- Written assignments and homework
- Project-based activities
- Group activities and presentations
- Practical tasks and hands-on activities
- Final examinations

### Skills Evaluated
- Number sense and place value understanding
- Computational fluency (addition, subtraction, multiplication basics)
- Problem-solving ability using multiple operations
- Conceptual understanding of mathematical concepts
- Application of learning in real-world contexts
- Measurement skills and estimation
- Data interpretation skills
- Pattern recognition and extension
- Mathematical reasoning and communication
- Accuracy and neatness in written work

## Important Notes

- Balance between concrete, pictorial, and abstract representation
- Regular practice of multiplication tables is essential
- Encourage mental math strategies for quick calculations
- Connect mathematics to real-life situations and daily experiences
- Use of manipulatives continues to be important for conceptual understanding
- Estimation skills should be developed alongside accurate calculation
- Word problems help develop reading comprehension and mathematical reasoning
- Regular feedback and positive reinforcement build confidence
- Homework should reinforce classroom learning, not introduce new concepts
- Cross-curricular integration with EVS, language, and art enhances learning
- Individual learning pace should be respected while maintaining curriculum progress
