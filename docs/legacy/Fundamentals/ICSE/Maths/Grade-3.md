# ICSE Grade 3 Mathematics Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Mathematics syllabus for Grade 3 extends students' mathematical knowledge to four-digit numbers and introduces foundational concepts of fractions. The curriculum deepens computational skills in all four operations while developing logical reasoning and problem-solving abilities. At this level, students are expected to demonstrate greater independence in applying mathematical concepts to real-world situations and multi-step problems.

---

## Theme 1: Numbers
### Content

**Numbers up to 9999 (Four-Digit Numbers)**
- Reading and writing four-digit numbers
- Number names from 1000 to 9999
- Understanding place value: thousands, hundreds, tens, ones
- Face value and place value in four-digit numbers
- Expanded form: e.g., 5,678 = 5000 + 600 + 70 + 8
- Short form and standard form of numbers
- Counting forward and backward (in ones, tens, hundreds, thousands)
- Successor and predecessor of a number
- Number line representation
- Comparison of four-digit numbers using >, <, =
- Ordering numbers (ascending and descending)
- Forming greatest and smallest numbers using given digits (with and without repetition)

**Place Value Concepts**
- Understanding the value of each digit in a number
- Decomposition and composition of numbers
- Regrouping numbers (e.g., 3 thousands = 30 hundreds)
- Building numbers using place value blocks
- Word problems involving place value

**Rounding and Estimation**
- Rounding numbers to nearest 10
- Rounding numbers to nearest 100
- Estimation of sums, differences, products
- Practical applications of estimation
- Checking reasonableness of answers

**Number Concepts**
- Even and odd numbers (up to 1000)
- Properties of even and odd numbers
- Skip counting by 2s, 3s, 4s, 5s, 6s, 7s, 8s, 9s, 10s, 25s, 50s, 100s
- Ordinal numbers: first to hundredth (1st to 100th)
- Cardinal and ordinal number relationship
- Number patterns and sequences

**Roman Numerals**
- Reading and writing Roman numerals from I to XX (1 to 20)
- Understanding symbols: I, V, X, L (1, 5, 10, 50)
- Conversion between Hindu-Arabic and Roman numerals
- Understanding the subtraction principle in Roman numerals (IV, IX)
- Word problems involving Roman numerals

## Theme 2: Number Operations
### Content

**Addition (up to 9999)**
- Addition without regrouping
- Addition with regrouping (carrying) across places
- Column addition of 3-digit and 4-digit numbers
- Addition of three or more numbers
- Properties of addition: commutative, associative, identity
- Estimation and verification of sums
- Mental addition strategies
- Word problems involving addition
- Real-life applications

**Subtraction (up to 9999)**
- Subtraction without regrouping
- Subtraction with regrouping (borrowing) across places
- Subtraction involving zeros
- Checking subtraction using addition
- Estimation and verification of differences
- Mental subtraction strategies
- Word problems involving subtraction
- Real-life applications

**Multiplication**
- Multiplication tables 2 to 20 (memorization and application)
- Multiplication of 2-digit numbers by 1-digit numbers
- Multiplication of 2-digit numbers by 2-digit numbers (introduction)
- Multiplication of 3-digit numbers by 1-digit numbers
- Properties of multiplication: commutative, associative, distributive
- Multiplication by 10, 100, and 1000
- Estimation of products
- Word problems involving multiplication
- Real-life applications

**Division**
- Understanding division as equal sharing and grouping
- Division as inverse of multiplication
- Division of 2-digit numbers by 1-digit numbers
- Division of 3-digit numbers by 1-digit numbers (introduction)
- Understanding quotient and remainder
- Division with remainder
- Checking division: Dividend = (Divisor × Quotient) + Remainder
- Division by 10, 100
- Word problems involving division
- Real-life applications

**Mixed Operations**
- Problems involving two or more operations
- Multi-step word problems
- Choosing appropriate operation for problem-solving
- Real-world problem-solving strategies

## Theme 3: Fractions
### Content

**Understanding Fractions**
- Concept of fraction as part of a whole
- Numerator and denominator
- Understanding fractions using objects and pictures
- Representation of fractions on number line
- Unit fractions: 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5, 1/6, 1/8, 1/10
- Reading and writing fractions

**Comparing Like-Denominator Fractions**
- Comparing fractions with same denominators
- Ordering like-denominator fractions from smallest to largest
- Using symbols >, <, = with fractions

**Fractions of a Collection**
- Finding fraction of a collection of objects (e.g., 1/3 of 12 objects)
- Word problems involving fractions of a collection

## Theme 4: Money
### Content

**Indian Currency**
- Recognition of all denominations (coins and notes)
- ₹1, ₹2, ₹5, ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100, ₹200, ₹500
- Understanding rupees and paise relationship (₹1 = 100 paise)
- Converting rupees to paise and vice versa
- Decimal notation for money (e.g., ₹45.50)

**Money Operations**
- Addition of money (up to ₹10,000)
- Subtraction of money (up to ₹10,000)
- Multiplication of money by whole numbers
- Division of money by whole numbers (simple cases)
- Making change and calculating balance
- Estimation in money calculations

**Practical Applications**
- Simple bills and receipts
- Shopping and calculating total cost
- Saving and spending
- Word problems involving money transactions
- Understanding value for money

## Theme 5: Measurement
### Content

**Length and Distance**
- Standard units: millimeter (mm), centimeter (cm), meter (m), kilometer (km)
- Conversion between units (mm ↔ cm ↔ m ↔ km)
- Measuring length using ruler and measuring tape
- Perimeter of regular and irregular shapes
- Perimeter of squares and rectangles using formulas
- Word problems involving length and perimeter
- Estimation and measurement in daily life

**Mass and Weight**
- Standard units: milligram (mg), gram (g), kilogram (kg)
- Conversion between units (g ↔ kg)
- Using weighing scales
- Word problems involving weight
- Estimation of mass

**Capacity and Volume**
- Standard units: milliliter (ml), liter (l)
- Conversion between units (ml ↔ l)
- Using measuring containers
- Word problems involving capacity
- Understanding volume concepts (introduction)

**Time**
- Reading analog clocks: hours, half hours, quarter hours, 5-minute intervals
- Reading digital clocks (12-hour and 24-hour format)
- AM and PM understanding
- Conversion: hours ↔ minutes ↔ seconds
- Duration and time intervals
- Calendar: days, weeks, months, years, leap years
- Date calculations and time problems
- Timetables and schedules
- Word problems involving time

**Temperature**
- Understanding temperature concept
- Reading thermometers
- Celsius scale (introduction)
- Hot and cold comparisons
- Real-life temperature contexts

- Intersecting and parallel lines (introduction)
- Horizontal and vertical lines
- Perpendicular lines (introduction)
- Right angles: recognition and identification
- Classifying angles as right or not-right

**2D Shapes (Polygons)**

- New shapes: pentagon, hexagon, heptagon, octagon
- Regular and irregular polygons
- Properties of shapes: sides, vertices, angles
- Perimeter of polygons
- Area concept (introduction)
- Area of squares and rectangles
- Symmetry: line symmetry, lines of symmetry in shapes
- Drawing symmetric figures

**3D Shapes (Solid Geometry)**
- Review: cube, cuboid, sphere, cone, cylinder
- New shapes: triangular prism, rectangular prism, pyramid
- Properties: faces, edges, vertices
- Real-world applications

**Patterns and Designs**
- Creating geometric patterns
- Tessellations and tiling patterns
- Symmetrical designs
- Rangoli and mandala patterns
- Geometric art

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 3, students should be able to:

✓ Read, write, and work with numbers up to 9999 confidently
✓ Understand and apply place value concepts for four-digit numbers
✓ Perform addition and subtraction with regrouping for numbers up to 9999
✓ Recall and apply multiplication tables from 2 to 20
✓ Multiply 2-digit by 2-digit numbers and 3-digit by 1-digit numbers
✓ Divide 3-digit numbers by 1-digit numbers with remainders
✓ Understand fractions: concept of a fraction, unit fractions
✓ Compare like-denominator fractions and find fractions of a collection
✓ Work with money including decimal notation and all operations
✓ Measure length, mass, capacity, and time using standard units
✓ Calculate perimeter and area of rectangles and squares
✓ Read and write Roman numerals up to XX
✓ Identify and describe properties of 2D and 3D shapes
✓ Recognize lines of symmetry in shapes
✓ Collect, organize, represent, and interpret data using various graphs
✓ Read and interpret pictographs, tally marks, and simple bar graphs
✓ Solve multi-step word problems using appropriate operations
✓ Demonstrate mental math skills and number sense
✓ Solve expressions involving mixed operations

## Important Notes

- Grade 3 marks a transition to more abstract mathematical thinking
- Regular practice of multiplication tables (2-20) is essential for fluency
- Fractions are introduced systematically—ensure strong conceptual foundation
- Mental math and estimation should be practiced daily
- Problem-solving strategies should be explicitly taught and modeled
- Use real-life contexts to make mathematics meaningful
- Encourage multiple solution strategies and mathematical discussions
- Differentiated instruction addresses varied learning needs
- Technology integration can enhance learning (when available)
- Cross-curricular connections strengthen understanding
- Regular home practice reinforces classroom learning
- Parent involvement and communication support student success
- Growth mindset and positive attitude toward mathematics should be fostered
