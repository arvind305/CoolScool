# ICSE Grade 5 Mathematics Syllabus

## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Mathematics syllabus for Grade 5 is designed to develop comprehensive mathematical understanding in students, building upon foundational concepts and introducing more complex problem-solving skills. The curriculum emphasizes conceptual understanding alongside computational fluency.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 1: Numbers

### Content

**Place Value and Number Sense**
- Reading and writing numbers up to 10,00,000 (ten lakhs / one million)
- Indian place value system: ones, tens, hundreds, thousands, ten thousands, lakhs, ten lakhs
- International place value system: ones through millions
- Face value and place value of digits in 7-digit numbers
- Expanded form and standard form of numbers
- Comparing numbers using <, >, =
- Ordering numbers in ascending and descending order

**Natural Numbers and Whole Numbers**
- Properties of whole numbers (starting from 0) and natural numbers (starting from 1)
- Successor and predecessor of a number
- Representation of numbers on a number line
- Skip counting by 2, 3, 4, 5, 10, 100
- Even numbers (divisible by 2) and odd numbers (not divisible by 2)

**Roman Numerals**
- Symbols: I, V, X, L, C, D, M (values 1, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500, 1000)
- Addition rule (smaller value after larger) and subtraction rule (IV, IX, XL, XC, CD, CM)
- Reading and writing Roman numerals up to 3,990 (MMMCMXC)
- Converting between Hindu-Arabic and Roman numerals

### Scope

- **In scope:** Numbers up to 10,00,000 (7 digits); Indian and International place value systems; expanded and standard forms; comparison and ordering; whole numbers and natural numbers on number line; successor/predecessor; even and odd numbers; skip counting; Roman numerals 1-3,990 with all seven symbols.
- **Not in scope:** Numbers beyond ten lakhs (crores); scientific notation; negative place values; binary or other number systems; Roman numeral arithmetic; vinculum (bar notation); numbers beyond 3,990 in Roman numerals.

### Learning Outcomes

- Read, write, compare and order numbers up to 10,00,000 in both Indian and International systems.
- Identify face value and place value of any digit in a 7-digit number.
- Express numbers in expanded form and convert back to standard form.
- Represent whole numbers on a number line and find successors and predecessors.
- Convert fluently between Hindu-Arabic and Roman numerals up to 3,990.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 2: Number Operations

### Content

**Addition and Subtraction**
- Addition and subtraction of numbers up to 10 lakhs
- Word problems involving addition and subtraction
- Estimating sums and differences
- Properties of addition: commutative and associative
- Checking answers using inverse operations

**Multiplication**
- Multiplication of numbers up to 5 digits by 3-digit numbers
- Lattice method and conventional method
- Properties of multiplication: commutative, associative, distributive
- Estimation of products
- Word problems based on multiplication
- Introduction to exponents (squares and cubes of small numbers)

**Division**
- Division of 5-digit numbers by 2-digit divisors
- Long division algorithm with remainders
- Estimation of quotients
- Checking division using multiplication
- Word problems involving division
- Relationship between dividend, divisor, quotient, and remainder

**Order of Operations**
- BODMAS/PEMDAS rule
- Solving expressions with mixed operations
- Using brackets and parentheses correctly

### Scope

- **In scope:** Addition and subtraction up to 7 digits; multiplication of up to 5-digit by 3-digit numbers; division of up to 5-digit by 2-digit numbers; BODMAS with whole numbers and mixed operations; properties of operations; estimation by rounding; squares and cubes of small numbers.
- **Not in scope:** Numbers beyond 10 lakhs in operations; multiplier beyond 3 digits; divisor beyond 2 digits; algebraic expressions; negative exponents or laws of exponents; BODMAS with fractions (complex); unitary method (not part of the syllabus).

### Learning Outcomes

- Perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division on large numbers with accuracy.
- Apply BODMAS rule to evaluate expressions with mixed operations and brackets.
- Use estimation to check reasonableness of answers.
- Recognise squares and cubes of small numbers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 3: Fractions and Decimals

### Content

**Fractions**
- Types: proper fractions, improper fractions, and mixed numbers; conversion between them
- Fraction notation and representation on a number line
- Equivalent fractions (by multiplication and division)
- Simplifying fractions to lowest terms
- Comparing and ordering fractions with same and different denominators (LCM method)
- Addition and subtraction of like and unlike fractions
- Multiplication of fractions by whole numbers
- Finding a fraction of a given quantity
- Word problems involving fractions

**Decimals**
- Decimals as fractions with denominator 10, 100, or 1,000
- Place value in decimals: tenths, hundredths, thousandths (up to 3 decimal places)
- Reading, writing, comparing, and ordering decimal numbers
- Converting between fractions and decimals (terminating decimals with denominator 10, 100, 1,000)
- Addition and subtraction of decimals (with place-value alignment)
- Multiplication and division of decimals by whole numbers
- Use of decimals in money (rupees and paise) and measurement contexts
- Word problems involving decimals

### Scope

- **In scope:** Proper, improper, and mixed fractions; fraction notation and representation on number line; equivalent fractions; simplification to lowest terms; addition and subtraction of like and unlike fractions; fraction x whole number; fraction of a quantity; decimals up to 3 places (thousandths); all comparison and ordering; addition, subtraction of decimals; multiplication and division of decimals by whole numbers; conversion between fractions and decimals; money and measurement applications.
- **Not in scope:** Fraction x fraction; fraction / fraction; complex or algebraic fractions; denominators beyond reasonable LCM; more than 3 decimal places; repeating/recurring decimals; decimal x decimal; decimal / decimal; scientific notation.

### Learning Outcomes

- Classify fractions as proper, improper, or mixed, and convert between forms.
- Find equivalent fractions and simplify to lowest terms.
- Add and subtract fractions with like and unlike denominators.
- Multiply a fraction by a whole number and find a fraction of a quantity.
- Read, write, compare, and order decimals up to thousandths.
- Perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of decimals by whole numbers.
- Solve word problems involving fractions and decimals in real-life contexts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 4: Playing with Numbers (Factors and Multiples)

### Content

**Factors and Multiples**
- Concept of factors (divides exactly) and multiples (product of a number and counting number)
- Finding all factors of a number systematically
- First 10 multiples of numbers up to 20
- Common factors of two numbers
- Prime numbers (exactly 2 factors) and composite numbers (more than 2 factors); 1 is neither
- Sieve of Eratosthenes for finding primes up to 100

**Divisibility Rules**
- Tests for divisibility by 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, and 10
- Applying divisibility rules to check factors and simplify calculations

**HCF and LCM**
- Prime factorization using factor tree and division methods
- Finding HCF (Highest Common Factor) by listing and by prime factorization
- Finding LCM (Lowest Common Multiple) by listing and by prime factorization
- Introduction to Euclid's algorithm (repeated subtraction/division)
- Relationship: HCF x LCM = Product of the two numbers
- Word problems: grouping, distribution, time intervals

### Scope

- **In scope:** Factors and multiples of numbers up to 1,000; prime and composite numbers; Sieve of Eratosthenes up to 100; divisibility rules for 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10; HCF and LCM of two or three numbers (up to 1,000) by listing, prime factorization, and basic Euclid's algorithm; word problems.
- **Not in scope:** Numbers beyond 1,000 for factor-finding; divisibility by 7, 11, or others; proofs of divisibility rules; modular arithmetic; HCF/LCM of more than 3 numbers; formal proof of Euclid's algorithm; extended Euclidean algorithm; twin primes or prime gaps.

### Learning Outcomes

- Find all factors and list multiples of given numbers.
- Classify numbers as prime or composite using the Sieve of Eratosthenes.
- Apply divisibility rules for 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, and 10.
- Compute HCF and LCM using prime factorization and verify using the product relationship.
- Solve real-life word problems involving HCF and LCM.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 5: Introduction to Negative Numbers

### Content

**Understanding Negative Numbers**
- Concept of negative numbers as numbers less than zero
- Real-life contexts: temperature below zero, debt/owing money, below sea level
- Representation of negative numbers on a number line
- Integers: ordering from least to greatest; comparing using <, >, =
- Simple addition and subtraction involving negative numbers (positive + negative, negative + positive)
- Word problems in real-life contexts

### Scope

- **In scope:** Negative numbers in context (temperature, money, elevation); integers on a number line; ordering and comparing integers; simple addition and subtraction of integers (range: -100 to +100); contextual word problems.
- **Not in scope:** Multiplication or division of negative numbers; formal absolute value; operations with multiple negative numbers; integers beyond -100 to +100 for operations.

### Learning Outcomes

- Explain negative numbers using real-life examples.
- Represent and locate integers on a number line.
- Compare and order positive and negative integers.
- Perform simple addition and subtraction involving negative numbers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 6: Geometry

### Content

**Basic Geometrical Ideas**
- Point, line, line segment, and ray; naming conventions
- Open and closed figures
- Interior, exterior, and boundary of a figure
- Curves: simple, closed, and non-closed

**Understanding Elementary Shapes (Angles)**
- Concept of an angle: vertex, arms, interior, exterior
- Types of angles: acute, right, obtuse, straight, reflex, complete
- Measuring angles using a protractor
- Pairs of angles: complementary (sum = 90 degrees) and supplementary (sum = 180 degrees)

**Polygons**
- Polygon as a closed figure with straight sides
- Triangles by sides: scalene, isosceles, equilateral
- Quadrilaterals: rectangle, square, parallelogram, rhombus, trapezium
- Properties of rectangles, squares, and other quadrilaterals
- Naming polygons by number of sides and angles: triangle (3), quadrilateral (4), pentagon (5), hexagon (6), heptagon (7), octagon (8)

**Circles**
- Basic concept and terminology: centre, radius, diameter, circumference
- Drawing circles with a given radius
- Properties of circles

**3D Shapes Introduction**
- Identification of common solids: cube, cuboid, cylinder, sphere, cone
- Faces, edges, and vertices of each solid
- Introduction to nets of 3D shapes (flat patterns that fold into solids)

### Scope

- **In scope:** Basic geometric terms (point, line, segment, ray); open/closed figures; all angle types and measurement with protractor; complementary and supplementary angles; classification of triangles and quadrilaterals with basic properties; naming polygons by number of sides and angles; circle terminology (centre, radius, diameter, circumference); drawing circles; properties of circles; 3D shapes (cube, cuboid, cylinder, sphere, cone) with faces, edges, vertices; nets of basic solids.
- **Not in scope:** Coordinate geometry; equations of lines; parallel/perpendicular line proofs; concurrent lines; angle bisector constructions; vertically opposite angles; angles on parallel lines; trigonometry; congruence and similarity; formal construction of polygons; diagonal properties; area of all polygons; circumference/area formulas for circles; arc, sector, tangent; chord; surface area and volume calculations; Euler's formula; symmetry (covered in Theme 10 Patterns); angle sum property of triangles/quadrilaterals; formal transformations (translation, rotation, reflection); tessellations.

### Learning Outcomes

- Identify and name basic geometric figures and use correct notation.
- Measure and classify angles; find complementary and supplementary angles.
- Classify triangles by sides and quadrilaterals by properties.
- Identify parts of a circle and draw circles with a compass.
- Identify common 3D shapes and count their faces, edges, and vertices.

## Theme 7: Measurement

### Content

**Length and Distance**
- Metric units: mm, cm, m, km
- Conversions: 1 km = 1,000 m; 1 m = 100 cm; 1 cm = 10 mm
- Measuring using rulers and appropriate tools
- Estimating lengths of common objects
- Addition and subtraction with unit conversion
- Word problems involving length

**Mass/Weight**
- Metric units: g, kg
- Conversion: 1 kg = 1,000 g
- Weighing using standard instruments
- Estimation of mass of common objects
- Word problems involving weight

**Capacity/Volume**
- Metric units: ml, l
- Conversion: 1 l = 1,000 ml
- Measuring using standard containers
- Estimation of capacity
- Word problems involving capacity

**Time**
- Reading and writing time in 12-hour (AM/PM) and 24-hour format
- Conversions: 1 hour = 60 minutes; 1 minute = 60 seconds
- Calendar: days, weeks, months, years
- Leap years and simple date calculations
- Time intervals and duration
- Word problems involving time

**Perimeter and Area**
- Perimeter as total boundary length
- Perimeter of rectangle = 2(l + b); perimeter of square = 4s
- Perimeter of irregular rectilinear shapes
- Area as space covered; square units (cm squared, m squared)
- Area of rectangle = l x b; area of square = s x s
- Relationship: same perimeter can yield different areas and vice versa
- Word problems involving perimeter and area

**Money**
- Indian currency: rupees and paise (1 rupee = 100 paise)
- Coins and notes; writing money in decimal form
- Converting between rupees and paise
- Addition and subtraction of money amounts
- Real-life word problems involving money

### Scope

- **In scope:** Metric conversions for length (mm, cm, m, km), mass (g, kg), and capacity (ml, l); estimation and measurement with standard instruments; 12-hour and 24-hour time; calendar (days, weeks, months, years) and leap years; elapsed time calculations; perimeter of rectangles, squares, and irregular shapes; area of rectangles and squares; relationship between perimeter and area; Indian currency operations (rupees and paise); word problems across all measurement topics.
- **Not in scope:** Imperial units; micrometer/nanometer; milligrams or tonnes; kiloliters or cubic cm relationships; volume of 3D shapes; time zones; speed-distance-time; area of triangles, parallelograms, or circles; surface area and volume; foreign currency or currency exchange; interest calculations; profit and loss (covered as application in Theme 8 Percentage).

### Learning Outcomes

- Convert between metric units of length, mass, and capacity.
- Read time in both 12-hour and 24-hour formats and calculate elapsed time.
- Calculate perimeter and area of rectangles and squares.
- Perform money calculations including conversions and real-life word problems.
- Solve measurement word problems in real-life contexts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 8: Introduction to Percentage

### Content

**Understanding Percentage**
- Percentage as "per hundred" (fraction with denominator 100)
- The % symbol and its usage
- Converting fractions to percentages (multiply by 100)
- Converting percentages to fractions (divide by 100, simplify)
- Converting decimals to percentages (multiply by 100)
- Finding a given percentage of a number (X% of a quantity)
- Simple applications: discounts, basic profit/loss as percentage
- Word problems involving percentages

### Scope

- **In scope:** Concept of percentage; notation; conversion between fractions, decimals, and percentages; finding X% of a number; simple discount calculations; basic profit = SP - CP and loss = CP - SP in context; word problems.
- **Not in scope:** Percentage increase/decrease; finding original value from a percentage; compound interest; successive percentages; percentage error.

### Learning Outcomes

- Explain percentage as "out of 100" and use the % symbol correctly.
- Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages.
- Calculate a given percentage of a quantity.
- Solve simple word problems involving percentages, discounts, and basic profit/loss.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Theme 9: Data Handling

### Content

**Collection and Organisation of Data**
- Methods of collecting data: survey, questionnaire, observation
- Organising data in tables
- Frequency distribution using tally marks (groups of 5)
- Creating frequency tables

**Representation of Data**
- Pictographs with symbols representing multiple units
- Bar graphs: reading and constructing with labelled axes
- Simple line graphs (reading)
- Pie charts: reading and interpreting (not constructing)
- Choosing the appropriate type of graph for given data

**Interpretation of Data**
- Reading information and extracting data from tables and graphs
- Comparing data; identifying highest, lowest, and trends
- Making simple conclusions from data
- Real-life data interpretation

**Basic Statistics**
- Mean (average) = sum of values / number of values
- Median = middle value when data is arranged in order (odd and even cases)
- Mode = most frequent value
- Simple problems involving mean, median, and mode
- Application in real-world contexts

### Scope

- **In scope:** Data collection through surveys; tally marks and frequency tables; pictographs with scales; bar graphs (reading and drawing); line graphs (reading); pie charts (reading only); choosing graph type; mean, median, and mode for small ungrouped data sets; word problems with averages; real-life interpretation.
- **Not in scope:** Sampling methods; grouped frequency distribution; class intervals; cumulative frequency; constructing pie charts; histograms; double bar graphs; scatter plots; box plots; weighted mean; range; standard deviation; bimodal/multimodal distributions; correlation; prediction/extrapolation; misleading graphs.

### Learning Outcomes

- Collect and organise data using tally marks and frequency tables.
- Read and construct pictographs and bar graphs; read line graphs and pie charts.
- Extract information from graphs and draw simple conclusions.
- Calculate mean, median, and mode of a small data set.
- Apply data handling skills to real-life situations.

## Theme 10: Patterns

### Content

**Number Patterns**
- Identifying number patterns
- Arithmetic sequences
- Finding the rule of a given pattern
- Finding missing terms and extending sequences
- Creating patterns from given rules

**Geometric Patterns**
- Visual patterns in shapes and designs
- Pattern extension in geometric figures
- Symmetry in patterns
- Line of symmetry
- Rotational symmetry (introductory)

**Patterns in Real Life**
- Patterns in nature (flowers, leaves, animals)
- Cultural patterns (rangoli, traditional designs, architecture)
- Creating original patterns

### Scope

- **In scope:** Arithmetic patterns (constant add/subtract/multiply/divide); arithmetic sequences; finding rules, missing terms, and next terms; creating patterns; visual/geometric patterns; extending shape patterns; symmetry in patterns; line of symmetry; rotational symmetry (introductory); real-life patterns in nature, culture, and architecture.
- **Not in scope:** Geometric sequences (formal); Fibonacci sequence; quadratic patterns; nth term formula; algebraic expressions for patterns; mathematical analysis of natural patterns; fractals; golden ratio; order of rotational symmetry; point symmetry; formal transformations.

### Learning Outcomes

- Identify and describe the rule governing a number or shape pattern.
- Find missing terms and extend given sequences.
- Create patterns based on specified rules.
- Identify lines of symmetry and understand rotational symmetry at an introductory level.
- Recognise and describe patterns in everyday life and nature.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Learning Outcomes (Summary)

By the end of Grade 5, students should be able to:

- Read, write, compare, and order numbers up to 10,00,000 in Indian and International systems.
- Perform all four operations on large numbers fluently, including estimation.
- Apply the BODMAS rule to evaluate multi-operation expressions.
- Work with fractions (add, subtract, multiply by whole number) and decimals (up to 3 places) confidently.
- Find factors, multiples, HCF, and LCM; apply divisibility rules for 2-10.
- Understand negative numbers and perform simple integer addition/subtraction.
- Identify and classify angles, triangles, quadrilaterals, circles, and 3D shapes.
- Measure and convert metric units of length, mass, capacity, and time.
- Calculate perimeter and area of rectangles and squares.
- Handle money calculations in real-life contexts.
- Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages; find a percentage of a quantity.
- Collect, organise, represent, and interpret data; calculate mean, median, and mode.
- Recognise and extend various patterns, including symmetry concepts.
- Solve word problems using multiple mathematical concepts.
