# ICSE Grade 6 Physics Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Physics syllabus for Grade 6 introduces students to fundamental concepts in physical science. This foundational year covers measurement, motion, force, light, sound, electricity, magnetism, and heat. The curriculum emphasizes observation, experimentation, and understanding of everyday phenomena through scientific principles.

---

## Theme 1: Measurement
### Content

**Need for Measurement**
- Importance of measurement in daily life
- Standard and non-standard units of measurement
- Limitations of non-standard units

**SI Units of Measurement**
- Introduction to the International System of Units (SI)
- Fundamental units: meter, kilogram, second

**Measurement of Length**
- SI unit of length: meter (m)
- Sub-units: centimeter (cm), millimeter (mm)
- Larger unit: kilometer (km)
- Tools: ruler, measuring tape
- Correct method of reading scales

**Measurement of Mass**
- SI unit of mass: kilogram (kg)
- Sub-unit: gram (g)
- Tools: beam balance, spring balance, electronic balance
- Difference between mass and weight (introduction)

**Measurement of Time**
- SI unit of time: second (s)
- Larger units: minute, hour, day
- Tools: clock, stopwatch
- Reading analog and digital clocks

## Theme 2: Motion
### Content

**Rest and Motion**
- Definition of rest: object at a fixed position
- Definition of motion: change in position with time
- Reference point: position from which motion is observed
- Motion is relative

**Types of Motion**
- Linear motion: movement in a straight line (car on highway)
- Circular motion: movement in a circle (hands of clock)
- Rotational motion: spinning around an axis (Earth's rotation)
- Oscillatory motion: to and fro movement (pendulum, swing)
- Random motion: irregular movement (butterfly, dust particles)
- Periodic motion: motion that repeats regularly

**Speed**
- Definition: distance covered per unit time
- Formula: Speed = Distance / Time
- SI unit: meter per second (m/s)
- Other unit: kilometer per hour (km/h)
- Slow and fast motion

**Distance and Time**
- Measuring distance traveled
- Recording time taken
- Relationship between speed, distance, and time

## Theme 3: Force
### Content

**Definition of Force**
- Force is a push or pull
- Force has magnitude and direction
- Force can be applied by contact or at a distance

**Effects of Force**
- Force can make a stationary object move
- Force can stop a moving object
- Force can change direction of motion
- Force can change speed of motion
- Force can change shape of an object

**Types of Forces**
- Muscular force: force exerted by muscles
- Frictional force: opposes motion between surfaces
- Gravitational force: pulls objects toward Earth
- Magnetic force: attraction or repulsion by magnets
- Electrostatic force: force between charged objects

**Contact and Non-contact Forces**
- Contact forces: require physical touch (friction, muscular)
- Non-contact forces: act at a distance (gravitational, magnetic, electrostatic)

## Theme 4: Light
### Content

**Sources of Light**
- Natural sources: sun, stars, moon (reflected), fireflies
- Artificial sources: candle, bulb, tube light, LED
- Luminous objects: produce their own light
- Non-luminous objects: do not produce light, may reflect light

**Properties of Light**
- Light travels in straight lines (rectilinear propagation)
- Light travels very fast

**Transparent, Translucent, and Opaque Objects**
- Transparent: allow light to pass through completely (glass, water, air)
- Translucent: allow some light to pass through (frosted glass, wax paper)
- Opaque: do not allow light to pass through (wood, metal, stone)

**Shadows**
- Formation of shadows: light blocked by opaque objects
- Requirements: light source, opaque object, screen
- Umbra: region of complete shadow
- Penumbra: region of partial shadow
- Factors affecting shadow size and shape

**Reflection of Light**
- Definition: bouncing back of light from a surface
- Smooth surfaces reflect light better
- Plane mirrors: flat reflecting surfaces
- Image characteristics: same size, laterally inverted, virtual, at same distance behind mirror

## Theme 5: Sound
### Content

**Production of Sound**
- Sound is produced by vibrating objects
- Vibration: rapid back and forth motion
- Examples: vocal cords, tuning fork, drum membrane, guitar strings

**Propagation of Sound**
- Sound requires a medium to travel
- Sound cannot travel through vacuum
- Sound travels through solids, liquids, and gases
- Speed of sound: fastest in solids, slower in liquids, slowest in gases

**Properties of Sound**
- Loudness: how loud or soft a sound is
- Depends on amplitude of vibration
- Pitch: how high or low a sound is
- Depends on frequency of vibration

**Musical Instruments**
- String instruments: vibrating strings produce sound
- Wind instruments: vibrating air columns produce sound
- Percussion instruments: vibrating membranes produce sound

---

## Theme 6: Electricity
### Content

**Electric Cell**
- Source of electrical energy
- Converts chemical energy to electrical energy
- Parts: positive terminal, negative terminal
- Types: dry cell, button cell

**Electric Circuit**
- Components: cell, connecting wires, bulb, switch
- Path through which electricity flows
- Circuit must be complete for current to flow

**Open and Closed Circuits**
- Closed circuit: complete path, current flows, bulb lights up
- Open circuit: broken path, no current flow, bulb does not light up
- Switch: used to open or close a circuit

**Conductors and Insulators**
- Conductors: materials that allow electricity to pass through
- Examples: metals (copper, aluminum, iron)
- Insulators: materials that do not allow electricity to pass through
- Examples: rubber, plastic, wood, glass

**Circuit Diagrams**
- Standard symbols for circuit components
- Drawing simple circuit diagrams
- Reading circuit diagrams

---

## Theme 7: Magnetism
### Content

**Magnets**
- Definition: objects that attract iron, nickel, cobalt
- Natural magnets: lodestone (magnetite)
- Artificial magnets: bar magnet, horseshoe magnet, cylindrical magnet

**Properties of Magnets**
- Magnets have two poles: north and south
- Poles are located at the ends where attraction is strongest
- Like poles repel each other
- Unlike poles attract each other
- A freely suspended magnet points north-south

**Magnetic and Non-magnetic Materials**
- Magnetic materials: iron, nickel, cobalt, steel
- Non-magnetic materials: wood, plastic, copper, aluminum, glass

**Uses of Magnets**
- Compass for navigation
- Refrigerator doors
- Magnetic toys
- Electric bells and speakers
- Credit cards and ATM cards
- Motors and generators

---

## Theme 8: Heat
### Content

**Sources of Heat**
- Sun: primary source of heat on Earth
- Fire and burning fuels
- Electricity
- Friction
- Human body

**Heat and Temperature**
- Heat: form of energy that flows from hot to cold objects
- Temperature: measure of hotness or coldness of an object
- Heat is measured in joules, temperature in degrees

**Measuring Temperature**
- Thermometer: device to measure temperature
- Clinical thermometer: measures body temperature (35-42 degrees C)
- Laboratory thermometer: measures wider range
- Celsius scale: water freezes at 0 degrees C, boils at 100 degrees C
- Fahrenheit scale: water freezes at 32 degrees F, boils at 212 degrees F

**Transfer of Heat**
- Conduction: heat transfer through solids by particle vibration
- Convection: heat transfer through fluids by particle movement
- Radiation: heat transfer without any medium

**Conductors and Insulators of Heat**
- Good conductors: metals (allow heat to pass easily)
- Poor conductors (insulators): wood, plastic, air, wool
- Applications: cooking vessels, thermos flask, woolen clothes

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 6 Physics, students should be able to:

- Understand the need for standard units and use SI units for measurement
- Measure length, mass, and time using appropriate instruments
- Identify different types of motion in everyday life
- Calculate speed using the formula Speed = Distance / Time
- Explain force as a push or pull and describe its effects
- Distinguish between contact and non-contact forces
- Identify natural and artificial sources of light
- Explain shadow formation and characteristics
- Describe reflection of light by plane mirrors
- Understand that sound is produced by vibrations and needs a medium
- Distinguish between loudness and pitch
- Construct simple electric circuits and identify components
- Classify materials as conductors or insulators
- Draw and interpret simple circuit diagrams
- Identify properties of magnets and magnetic materials
- Explain attraction and repulsion between magnetic poles
- Distinguish between heat and temperature
- Explain the three modes of heat transfer
- Classify materials as conductors or insulators of heat

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 80 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 20 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Very Short Answer (MCQs and one-word answers) - 20 marks
- Section B: Short Answer (definitions, explanations) - 30 marks
- Section C: Long Answer (detailed explanations, diagrams) - 30 marks

### Practical Activities
- Measuring length using ruler and measuring tape
- Observing different types of motion
- Demonstrating effects of force
- Forming shadows with different objects
- Observing reflection in plane mirrors
- Producing sound using different objects
- Building simple electric circuits
- Testing conductors and insulators
- Exploring properties of magnets
- Demonstrating heat transfer
