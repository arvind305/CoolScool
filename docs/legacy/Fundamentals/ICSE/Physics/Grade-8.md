# ICSE Grade 8 Physics Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Physics syllabus for Grade 8 provides a comprehensive foundation in physical sciences, covering fundamental concepts of matter, measurement, force, energy, light, heat, sound, and electricity. The curriculum emphasizes conceptual understanding through practical experiments and real-world applications, preparing students for advanced physics in secondary classes.

---

## Theme 1: Matter
### Content

**Matter (Substance)**
- Definition of matter and its properties
- Physical nature and chemical properties
- Experiments demonstrating matter has mass and occupies space

**Characteristics of Particles (Molecules) of Matter**
- Molecules as building blocks of matter
- Classification of matter based on molecular arrangement

**States of Matter**
- Comparison of solids, liquids, and gases
- Characteristics of the solid state: fixed shape and volume, closely packed molecules
- Characteristics of the liquid state: fixed volume, takes shape of container
- Characteristics of the gaseous state: no fixed shape or volume, widely spaced molecules
- Kinetic theory of matter

**Change of State**
- Melting and freezing: definition, melting point, freezing point
- Evaporation and boiling: differences and factors affecting
- Condensation and desublimation
- Sublimation: direct solid to gas conversion
- Energy changes during state changes
- Latent heat concept (introduction)

---

## Theme 2: Physical Quantities and Measurement
### Content

**Density**
- Definition of density (mass per unit volume)
- SI unit: kg/m³ and g/cm³
- Formula: Density = Mass / Volume
- Relation between density and floating/sinking

**Volume Measurement**
- Measuring volume of regular solids (using formulas)
- Measuring volume of irregular solids (water displacement method)
- Measuring cylinders and graduated vessels

**Density Determination**
- Determining density of regular solids
- Determining density of irregular solids
- Determining density of liquids

**Relative Density**
- Definition: ratio of density of substance to density of water
- Relative density has no units
- Applications of relative density

**Buoyancy and Upthrust**
- Definition of buoyancy
- Upthrust force: direction and magnitude
- Factors affecting buoyancy

**Principle of Floatation**
- Archimedes' principle: statement and explanation
- Conditions for floating and sinking
- Applications: ships, submarines, hydrometers, hot air balloons

---

## Theme 3: Force and Pressure
### Content

**Force**
- Definition of force as push or pull
- Effects of force: change in motion, shape, direction
- Types of forces: contact and non-contact
- Formula: Force = Mass × Acceleration
- SI unit: Newton (N)

**Moment of Force (Torque)**
- Definition of moment of force
- Formula: Moment = Force × Perpendicular distance
- SI unit: Newton-meter (N·m)
- Clockwise and anticlockwise moments
- Principle of moments
- Applications: levers, see-saw, door handles

**Thrust and Pressure**
- Definition of thrust
- Definition of pressure: force per unit area
- Formula: Pressure = Force / Area
- SI unit: Pascal (Pa)
- Relationship between pressure and area

**Pressure in Liquids**
- Pressure at a point in a liquid
- Factors affecting liquid pressure: depth, density
- Formula: P = ρgh (introduction)
- Pressure acts equally in all directions at a point
- Pascal's law and applications

**Atmospheric Pressure**
- Definition and existence of atmospheric pressure
- Torricelli's experiment (concept)
- Barometer and measurement of atmospheric pressure
- Value of standard atmospheric pressure
- Applications: drinking straw, syringe, suction cups

---

## Theme 4: Energy
### Content

**Work**
- Scientific definition of work
- Work is done when force causes displacement
- Formula: Work = Force × Displacement × cos θ
- SI unit: Joule (J)
- Positive, negative, and zero work

**Energy**
- Definition of energy: capacity to do work
- SI unit: Joule (J)
- Forms of energy: mechanical, heat, light, sound, electrical, chemical, nuclear

**Mechanical Energy**
- Potential energy: energy due to position or configuration
- Gravitational potential energy: PE = mgh
- Kinetic energy: energy due to motion
- Formula: KE = ½mv²
- Types of kinetic energy: translational, rotational, vibrational

**Energy Conversion**
- Law of conservation of energy
- Energy transformations in daily life
- Examples: pendulum, falling objects, electrical appliances

**Solar Energy**
- Sun as the primary source of energy
- Solar energy applications
- Renewable energy concept

**Power**
- Definition: rate of doing work
- Formula: Power = Work / Time
- SI unit: Watt (W)
- Larger units: kilowatt (kW), megawatt (MW)
- Calculation of power in practical situations

---

## Theme 5: Light Energy
### Content

**Speed of Light**
- Light travels in straight lines
- Speed of light in vacuum: 3 × 10⁸ m/s
- Light travels at different speeds in different media

**Refraction of Light**
- Definition of refraction
- Cause of refraction: change in speed
- Laws of refraction (Snell's law - qualitative)
- Refractive index concept
- Refraction through rectangular glass slab
- Lateral displacement

**Atmospheric Refraction**
- Twinkling of stars
- Apparent position of sun at sunrise and sunset
- Mirage formation

**Refraction Through Prism**
- Path of light through a triangular prism
- Angle of deviation
- Dispersion of light
- Spectrum: VIBGYOR
- Rainbow formation

**Spherical Mirrors**
- Types: concave and convex mirrors
- Terms: pole, centre of curvature, radius of curvature, principal axis, focus, focal length
- Relation: R = 2f
- Image formation by concave mirrors
- Image formation by convex mirrors
- Mirror formula (introduction): 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
- Uses of concave and convex mirrors

---

## Theme 6: Heat Transfer
### Content

**Heat**
- Definition of heat as a form of energy
- Units of heat: Joule, calorie
- Relationship: 1 calorie = 4.186 J
- Difference between heat and temperature

**Effects of Heat**
- Change in temperature
- Change in state
- Change in dimensions (expansion)

**Thermal Expansion**
- Expansion of solids: linear, superficial, cubical
- Coefficients of expansion (concept)
- Expansion of liquids: real and apparent expansion
- Anomalous expansion of water
- Expansion of gases
- Applications of thermal expansion

**Density Variation with Temperature**
- Effect of heating on density
- Applications in convection currents

---

## Theme 7: Sound
### Content

**Sound Waves**
- Sound as a form of energy
- Sound is produced by vibrations
- Sound requires a medium to travel
- Sound cannot travel through vacuum
- Speed of sound in different media

**Characteristics of Sound**
- Loudness: related to amplitude
- Intensity: energy per unit area
- Pitch: related to frequency
- Quality (timbre): distinguishes sounds of same pitch and loudness
- Frequency: number of vibrations per second
- SI unit: Hertz (Hz)

**Wave Representation**
- Wavelength, amplitude, frequency
- Relationship: v = fλ
- Graphical representation of sound waves
- Compression and rarefaction

**Production and Propagation**
- Sources of sound: vibrating objects
- Propagation through air as longitudinal waves
- Echo and reverberation (introduction)

---

## Theme 8: Electricity
### Content

**Household Electricity**
- Electric power transmission
- AC and DC (basic difference)
- Domestic electric circuits
- Wire color coding: live (red/brown), neutral (black/blue), earth (green)
- Electric meter reading

**Circuit Components**
- Fuses: purpose and working
- Circuit breakers
- Earthing: importance and method
- Household circuits: series and parallel connections
- Power ratings of appliances
- Safety precautions with electricity

**Static Electricity**
- Electric charge: positive and negative
- Charging by friction
- Charge transfer methods: conduction, induction
- Conductors, insulators, and semiconductors
- Electroscope: construction and working

**Lightning**
- Cause of lightning
- Lightning conductor
- Safety measures during thunderstorms

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 8 Physics, students should be able to:

✓ Explain the kinetic theory of matter and changes of state
✓ Understand density, relative density, and their applications
✓ Apply Archimedes' principle to explain floating and sinking
✓ Calculate moments and apply the principle of moments
✓ Understand pressure in solids, liquids, and gases
✓ Define work, energy, and power with their relationships
✓ Apply conservation of energy in various situations
✓ Explain refraction of light and its applications
✓ Describe image formation by mirrors and lenses
✓ Understand dispersion and spectrum of light
✓ Explain thermal expansion and its applications
✓ Describe characteristics of sound waves
✓ Understand household electricity and safety measures
✓ Explain static electricity and lightning

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 80 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 20 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Very Short Answer (MCQs and one-word answers) - 20 marks
- Section B: Short Answer (definitions, explanations, numericals) - 30 marks
- Section C: Long Answer (detailed explanations, diagrams, derivations) - 30 marks

### Practical Activities
- Experiments on density and floatation
- Verification of Archimedes' principle
- Study of pressure in liquids
- Experiments on reflection and refraction
- Study of thermal expansion
- Experiments on sound production and characteristics
