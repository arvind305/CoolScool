# ICSE Grade 1 Science (EVS - Environmental Studies) Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Environmental Studies (EVS) syllabus for Grade 1 introduces young learners to the world around them through simple observations and experiences. The curriculum covers fundamental concepts about self, family, plants, animals, food, water, air, weather, and good habits. Learning is activity-based, visual, and connected to everyday life to make it meaningful for 6-7 year old children.

---

## Theme 1: My Body
### Content

**Body Parts**
- Head, eyes, ears, nose, mouth
- Hands, legs, fingers, toes
- Hair, face, neck, shoulders
- Naming and pointing to body parts

**Five Senses**
- Sense of sight: eyes
- Sense of hearing: ears
- Sense of smell: nose
- Sense of taste: tongue
- Sense of touch: skin

**Functions of Body Parts**
- Eyes help us see
- Ears help us hear
- Nose helps us smell
- Tongue helps us taste
- Skin helps us feel (hot, cold, soft, hard)
- Hands help us hold and do things
- Legs help us walk, run, jump

**Taking Care of Our Body**
- Washing hands and face
- Brushing teeth
- Taking bath daily
- Combing hair
- Cutting nails

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify and name major body parts
- Know the five senses and their organs
- Understand simple functions of body parts
- Learn basic body care habits

---

## Theme 2: My Family and Home
### Content

**Family Members**
- Mother, father
- Brother, sister
- Grandparents (grandfather, grandmother)
- Loving and caring for each other

**Types of Families**
- Small family (fewer members)
- Big family or joint family (many members)
- All families are special

**Parts of a House**
- Bedroom: for sleeping
- Kitchen: for cooking
- Bathroom: for bathing
- Living room: for sitting together
- Dining room: for eating

**Things We Need at Home**
- Basic needs: food, clothes, shelter
- Furniture: bed, chair, table
- Kitchen items: pots, pans, plates
- Keeping our home clean

### Learning Outcomes
- Name family members and understand relationships
- Differentiate between small and big families
- Identify rooms in a house and their uses
- Understand basic needs and household items

---

## Theme 3: Plants Around Us
### Content

**Parts of a Plant**
- Root: underground, takes water from soil
- Stem: holds the plant up
- Leaf: green, makes food
- Flower: colorful, beautiful
- Fruit: contains seeds

**Types of Plants**
- Trees: big and tall (mango, neem, banyan)
- Shrubs: smaller than trees (rose, hibiscus)
- Herbs: very small plants (mint, tulsi)
- Creepers: grow along the ground (pumpkin, watermelon)
- Climbers: climb up walls or trees (money plant, grape vine)

**Uses of Plants**
- Give us food: fruits, vegetables
- Give us medicines
- Give us wood
- Give us shade
- Give us fresh air (oxygen)
- Make our surroundings beautiful

**Caring for Plants**
- Water them regularly
- Give them sunlight
- Do not pluck leaves or flowers unnecessarily
- Plant more trees

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify parts of a plant
- Classify plants by size
- List uses of plants
- Understand how to care for plants

---

## Theme 4: Animals Around Us
### Content

**Pet Animals**
- Dog: guards our home, barks
- Cat: catches mice, meows
- Fish: lives in water, swims
- Parrot: talks, has colorful feathers
- Rabbit: soft fur, hops

**Farm Animals**
- Cow: gives milk, moos
- Hen: gives eggs, clucks
- Goat: gives milk, bleats
- Horse: helps carry load, neighs
- Sheep: gives wool, baas

**Wild Animals**
- Lion: king of jungle, roars
- Tiger: striped, roars
- Elephant: big, has trunk
- Monkey: climbs trees
- Deer: runs fast, has antlers

**Animal Homes**
- Nest: for birds
- Den: for lion
- Kennel: for dog
- Stable: for horse
- Coop: for hen
- Burrow: for rabbit

**What Animals Eat**
- Some animals eat plants (cow, elephant, deer)
- Some animals eat other animals (lion, tiger)
- Some animals eat both (dog, cat)

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify pet, farm, and wild animals
- Know animal sounds
- Match animals with their homes
- Understand what animals eat

---

## Theme 5: Food We Eat
### Content

**Types of Food**
- Fruits: apple, banana, mango, orange
- Vegetables: carrot, potato, spinach, tomato
- Grains: rice, wheat, bread, roti
- Dairy: milk, curd, cheese
- Eggs and meat

**Sources of Food**
- Food from plants: fruits, vegetables, rice, wheat, oil
- Food from animals: milk, eggs, meat, honey

**Healthy and Unhealthy Food**
- Healthy food: fruits, vegetables, milk, dal, rice
- Unhealthy food: too much chips, candy, cold drinks
- Junk food: eat only sometimes
- Eating a balanced diet

**Meals of the Day**
- Breakfast: first meal, gives energy for the day
- Lunch: midday meal
- Dinner: evening meal
- Healthy snacks between meals

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify different types of food
- Know sources of food (plants and animals)
- Distinguish healthy from unhealthy food
- Understand the importance of meals

---

## Theme 6: Water
### Content

**Uses of Water**
- Drinking: keeps us healthy
- Cooking: to make food
- Bathing: to stay clean
- Washing: clothes and dishes
- Watering plants
- For animals to drink

**Sources of Water**
- Tap water at home
- Well: deep hole with water
- River: flowing water
- Rain: water from clouds
- Pond and lake

**Saving Water**
- Turn off taps when not in use
- Do not waste water while brushing
- Take short baths
- Water plants with just enough water
- Water is precious

**Clean and Dirty Water**
- Clean water: safe to drink
- Dirty water: has germs, makes us sick
- Always drink clean, boiled or filtered water
- Do not throw garbage in water

### Learning Outcomes
- List uses of water
- Identify sources of water
- Understand the importance of saving water
- Know the difference between clean and dirty water

---

## Theme 7: Air
### Content

**Air is Everywhere**
- We cannot see air
- Air is all around us
- Air fills balloons and bubbles
- We breathe air

**Uses of Air**
- We need air to breathe
- All living things need air
- Air helps birds fly
- Air fills tires and balloons
- Air dries our clothes

**Moving Air (Wind)**
- Moving air is called wind
- Wind can be gentle or strong
- Wind flies kites
- Wind turns windmills
- Wind cools us on hot days

**Clean and Dirty Air**
- Clean air: fresh and healthy
- Dirty air: smoke, dust, pollution
- Trees give clean air
- Vehicles and factories make air dirty
- We should keep air clean

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand that air is everywhere
- List uses of air
- Know that wind is moving air
- Differentiate clean and polluted air

---

## Theme 8: Weather and Seasons
### Content

**Types of Weather**
- Sunny: bright and warm
- Rainy: water falls from clouds
- Cloudy: clouds cover the sky
- Windy: air moves fast
- Cold: temperature is low
- Hot: temperature is high

**Seasons of the Year**
- Summer: hot weather, long days
- Winter: cold weather, short days
- Rainy season (Monsoon): lots of rain
- Spring and Autumn: mild weather

**Clothes for Different Weather**
- Summer: light clothes, cotton, shorts
- Winter: sweaters, jackets, caps, gloves
- Rainy: raincoat, umbrella, boots
- Wear clothes that match the weather

**Activities in Different Seasons**
- Summer: swimming, eating ice cream, mangoes
- Winter: playing in sun, drinking hot drinks
- Rainy: playing in rain, jumping in puddles
- Festivals in different seasons

### Learning Outcomes
- Describe different types of weather
- Name the main seasons
- Choose appropriate clothes for weather
- Know seasonal activities

---

## Theme 9: Day and Night
### Content

**The Sun and Daytime**
- Sun rises in the morning
- Sun gives us light and heat
- Daytime is bright
- Sun sets in the evening
- Sun is very hot, do not look directly

**The Moon and Nighttime**
- Moon appears at night
- Stars twinkle in the sky
- Night is dark
- Moon gives gentle light
- Moon changes shape

**Day Activities**
- Wake up in the morning
- Brush teeth, take bath
- Eat breakfast
- Go to school
- Play with friends
- Study and do homework

**Night Activities**
- Have dinner
- Spend time with family
- Listen to stories
- Go to sleep
- Rest and dream
- Some animals wake up at night (owl, bat)

### Learning Outcomes
- Know that Sun gives light during day
- Know that Moon and stars appear at night
- List daily activities
- Understand day and night routine

---

## Theme 10: Good Habits
### Content

**Personal Hygiene**
- Brush teeth twice a day (morning and night)
- Take bath every day
- Wash hands before eating
- Wash hands after using toilet
- Keep nails short and clean
- Comb hair neatly

**Safety Habits**
- Look both ways before crossing road
- Hold an adult's hand while crossing
- Do not play with fire or sharp objects
- Do not talk to strangers
- Tell parents where you are going
- Do not run on stairs

**Good Manners**
- Say "Please" when asking for something
- Say "Thank you" when receiving something
- Say "Sorry" when you make a mistake
- Say "Excuse me" politely
- Share with others
- Wait for your turn
- Respect elders

**Healthy Habits**
- Eat healthy food
- Drink plenty of water
- Sleep on time (8-10 hours)
- Exercise and play every day
- Do not eat too many sweets
- Sit and stand properly

### Learning Outcomes
- Practice personal hygiene
- Follow safety rules
- Use good manners
- Develop healthy habits

## Assessment

### Components
- Continuous Assessment throughout the year
- Oral questions and responses
- Activity-based assessment
- Picture-based worksheets
- Total: 100 marks (Internal Assessment)

### Assessment Pattern for Class 1
- Observation: Participation in class activities
- Oral Assessment: Simple questions about topics
- Picture Worksheets: Identify, match, color
- Projects: Simple drawings, collections

### Practical Activities
- Pointing to body parts
- Sorting fruits and vegetables
- Identifying animals from pictures
- Observing plants in the garden
- Weather chart (sunny, rainy, cloudy)
- Show and tell about family

## Teaching Guidelines

- Use simple language that 6-7 year olds can understand
- Make learning fun with songs, rhymes, and games
- Use pictures and real objects whenever possible
- Connect topics to children's daily experiences
- Encourage observation and asking questions
- Praise effort and participation
- No written exams; assessment through activities

---

## Important Notes

- Grade 1 EVS is activity-based and experiential
- Focus on observation and exploration
- Build vocabulary related to environment
- Develop awareness about self and surroundings
- Emphasize good habits and values
- Make connections between topics (e.g., plants need water, air)
- Safety and hygiene are important themes
- Learning should be joyful and stress-free
