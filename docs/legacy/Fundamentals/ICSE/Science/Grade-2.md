# ICSE Grade 2 Science (EVS - Environmental Studies) Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Environmental Studies (EVS) syllabus for Grade 2 introduces young learners to the world around them through engaging, age-appropriate content. The curriculum covers the human body, living and non-living things, plants, animals, food, water, air, weather, Earth, safety, and environment. The approach emphasizes observation, exploration, and developing healthy habits.

---

## Theme 1: Our Body
### Content

**External Body Parts**
- Head, hands, legs, eyes, ears, nose, mouth
- Functions of external body parts
- Taking care of body parts

**Internal Organs (Introduction)**
- Heart: pumps blood
- Lungs: help us breathe
- Stomach: digests food
- Brain: helps us think

**Sense Organs**
- Eyes: help us see
- Ears: help us hear
- Nose: helps us smell
- Tongue: helps us taste
- Skin: helps us feel (touch)

**Taking Care of Our Body**
- Daily hygiene: brushing teeth, bathing, washing hands
- Exercise and physical activity
- Proper rest and sleep
- Eating healthy food

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify and name external body parts
- Know the main functions of sense organs
- Understand basic internal organs and their jobs
- Practice good hygiene habits

---

## Theme 2: Living and Non-Living Things
### Content

**Characteristics of Living Things**
- Living things need food and water
- Living things breathe air
- Living things grow and change
- Living things move on their own
- Living things reproduce (have babies)

**Characteristics of Non-Living Things**
- Non-living things do not eat or drink
- Non-living things do not breathe
- Non-living things do not grow
- Non-living things do not move on their own
- Non-living things do not have babies

**Classification**
- Identifying living things around us (plants, animals, humans)
- Identifying non-living things around us (toys, stones, furniture)
- Sorting objects into living and non-living categories

### Learning Outcomes
- Differentiate between living and non-living things
- List characteristics of living things
- Classify everyday objects correctly

---

## Theme 3: Plants Around Us
### Content

**Parts of a Plant**
- Root: absorbs water and minerals, holds plant in soil
- Stem: carries water to leaves, supports the plant
- Leaf: makes food for the plant
- Flower: helps plant make seeds
- Fruit: contains seeds
- Seed: grows into a new plant

**Types of Plants**
- Trees: tall, thick stem (trunk), e.g., mango, neem, banyan
- Shrubs: medium height, many stems, e.g., rose, hibiscus
- Herbs: small, soft stems, e.g., tulsi, mint, coriander
- Climbers: need support to climb, e.g., money plant, grapevine
- Creepers: spread along the ground, e.g., pumpkin, watermelon

**What Plants Need**
- Water
- Sunlight
- Air
- Soil (nutrients)

### Learning Outcomes
- Name and identify parts of a plant
- Explain the function of each plant part
- Classify plants into trees, shrubs, herbs, climbers, creepers
- Understand what plants need to grow

---

## Theme 4: Animals Around Us
### Content

**Where Animals Live (Habitats)**
- Land animals: dog, cat, lion, elephant, cow
- Water animals: fish, whale, dolphin, octopus
- Animals that live on land and water: frog, crocodile, turtle

**What Animals Eat (Food Habits)**
- Herbivores: eat only plants (cow, rabbit, elephant)
- Carnivores: eat only meat (lion, tiger, shark)
- Omnivores: eat both plants and meat (bear, crow, dog)

**Animals and Their Young Ones**
- Cow - Calf
- Dog - Puppy
- Cat - Kitten
- Hen - Chick
- Duck - Duckling
- Frog - Tadpole

**Animal Body Coverings**
- Fur: dog, cat, bear
- Feathers: birds (hen, parrot, peacock)
- Scales: fish, snake
- Shell: turtle, snail

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify animals by their habitat
- Classify animals by their food habits
- Match animals with their young ones
- Identify different body coverings

---

## Theme 5: Food We Eat
### Content

**Sources of Food**
- Food from plants: fruits, vegetables, grains, pulses
- Food from animals: milk, eggs, meat, honey

**Types of Food**
- Fruits: apple, banana, orange, mango
- Vegetables: carrot, potato, spinach, tomato
- Grains: rice, wheat, corn
- Dairy: milk, cheese, butter, curd
- Pulses: dal, beans, chickpeas

**Healthy Eating**
- Eating different types of food every day
- Importance of fruits and vegetables
- Drinking enough water
- Avoiding junk food

**Raw and Cooked Food**
- Raw food: fruits, salads, some vegetables
- Cooked food: rice, roti, vegetables, dal
- Why we cook food (to make it tasty, safe, easy to eat)

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify sources of different foods
- Classify foods into categories
- Understand the importance of balanced eating
- Differentiate between raw and cooked food

---

## Theme 6: Water
### Content

**Uses of Water**
- Drinking
- Cooking
- Bathing and cleaning
- Watering plants
- Washing clothes

**Sources of Water**
- Rain
- Rivers and lakes
- Wells and handpumps
- Taps (piped water)

**The Water Cycle (Simple)**
- Sun heats water in rivers and seas
- Water becomes vapor and goes up
- Vapor forms clouds
- Clouds bring rain

**Saving Water**
- Turn off taps when not in use
- Do not waste water while brushing
- Use bucket instead of shower
- Fix leaking taps
- Reuse water when possible

### Learning Outcomes
- List uses of water in daily life
- Identify different sources of water
- Explain the simple water cycle
- Practice water conservation habits

---

## Theme 7: Air and Weather
### Content

**Air Around Us**
- Air is everywhere
- Air is invisible (we cannot see it)
- Air has no color or smell (pure air)
- Moving air is called wind

**Uses and Importance of Air**
- All living things need air to breathe
- Air helps birds fly
- Air helps dry clothes
- Air moves windmills and sails boats
- Air fills balloons

**Types of Weather**
- Sunny: bright sun, warm or hot
- Rainy: clouds, rain falls
- Cloudy: sky covered with clouds, no sun
- Windy: strong wind blows

**Seasons**
- Summer: hot weather, light clothes
- Rainy (Monsoon): lots of rain, raincoat and umbrella
- Winter: cold weather, warm clothes

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand that air is all around us
- List uses of air
- Identify different types of weather
- Describe activities and clothes for each season

---

## Theme 8: Our Earth
### Content

**Land and Water on Earth**
- Earth has both land and water
- Land areas: continents
- Water areas: oceans and seas

**Landforms**
- Mountains: very high land
- Hills: smaller than mountains
- Plains: flat land
- Valleys: low land between mountains/hills
- Deserts: dry land with sand

**Water Bodies**
- Rivers: long flowing water
- Lakes: water surrounded by land
- Seas and Oceans: very large and salty water

**Day and Night**
- Sun gives us light and heat
- Day: when Sun shines on us
- Night: when Sun does not shine on us
- Moon shines at night

### Learning Outcomes
- Know that Earth has land and water
- Identify different landforms
- Name different water bodies
- Understand day and night

---

## Theme 9: Safety Rules
### Content

**Road Safety**
- Always walk on the footpath
- Look both ways before crossing
- Use zebra crossing or footbridge
- Follow traffic lights:
  - Red: Stop
  - Yellow: Wait
  - Green: Go
- Never play on the road

**Safety at Home**
- Do not touch fire or hot things
- Do not play with sharp objects (knife, scissors)
- Do not touch electrical sockets
- Do not run on stairs
- Keep away from medicines and chemicals

**Safety at School and Playground**
- Do not push or fight
- Walk, do not run in corridors
- Use playground equipment safely
- Follow teacher's instructions

**Stranger Safety**
- Do not talk to strangers
- Do not take food or gifts from strangers
- Do not go anywhere with strangers
- Tell a trusted adult if someone makes you uncomfortable

### Learning Outcomes
- Follow road safety rules
- Practice safety at home
- Stay safe at school and playground
- Know how to stay safe from strangers

---

## Theme 10: Our Environment
### Content

**Keeping Surroundings Clean**
- Keep home, school, and neighborhood clean
- Do not litter
- Use dustbins for garbage
- Clean surroundings keep us healthy

**Waste Disposal**
- Wet waste: food scraps, fruit peels
- Dry waste: paper, plastic, glass
- Put garbage in the correct bin
- Do not throw garbage in water or on roads

**Caring for Plants and Animals**
- Water plants regularly
- Do not pluck flowers unnecessarily
- Plant more trees
- Feed and care for pet animals
- Do not harm any animal

**The Three Rs**
- Reduce: use less (less plastic bags)
- Reuse: use again (old bottles as flower pots)
- Recycle: make new things from old (paper recycling)

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand the importance of cleanliness
- Dispose of garbage properly
- Care for plants and animals
- Practice reduce, reuse, recycle

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Assessment: 40 marks
- Practical/Project Work: 30 marks
- Oral Assessment: 20 marks
- Participation: 10 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Assessment Methods
- Picture-based questions
- Fill in the blanks
- Match the following
- True/False
- Short answer questions
- Drawing and labeling
- Activity-based assessment

### Suggested Activities
- Growing a plant from seed
- Sorting living and non-living things
- Making a chart of body parts
- Weather observation diary
- Creating a healthy food plate
- Road safety drama/role play
- Making a dustbin for the classroom

## Important Notes

- Grade 2 EVS focuses on observation and exploration
- Use simple language appropriate for 7-8 year olds
- Connect learning to everyday life experiences
- Encourage hands-on activities and projects
- Develop good habits through practice
- Make learning fun with songs, stories, and games
- Involve parents in reinforcing concepts at home
