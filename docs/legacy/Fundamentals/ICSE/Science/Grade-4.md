# ICSE Grade 4 Science (EVS - Environmental Studies) Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Environmental Studies (EVS) syllabus for Grade 4 expands students' understanding of the natural world through exploration of human body systems, plant and animal life cycles, matter and energy concepts, and environmental awareness. The curriculum encourages scientific inquiry, observation, and application of knowledge to everyday situations appropriate for 9-10 year old learners.

---

## Theme 1: Human Body - Skeletal and Muscular Systems
### Content

**The Skeleton**
- Functions of the skeletal system
- Support: holds up the body
- Protection: protects internal organs
- Movement: works with muscles
- Blood cell production: in bone marrow

**Major Bones**
- Skull: protects the brain
- Rib cage: protects heart and lungs
- Spine (backbone): made of vertebrae, supports body, allows bending
- Arm bones: humerus, radius, ulna
- Leg bones: femur, tibia, fibula
- Hip bone and shoulder blade

**Joints**
- Where two or more bones meet
- Hinge joints: knee, elbow (open and close like a door)
- Ball-and-socket joints: shoulder, hip (move in all directions)
- Pivot joints: neck (allows rotation)
- Fixed joints: skull (do not move)

**Muscles**
- Muscles are attached to bones by tendons
- Muscles work in pairs
- One muscle contracts (shortens), the other relaxes
- Muscles help in movement

**Caring for Bones and Muscles**
- Eat calcium-rich foods: milk, cheese, yogurt
- Vitamin D from sunlight
- Regular exercise
- Good posture
- Avoid injuries

### Learning Outcomes
- Explain functions of the skeletal system
- Identify major bones in the body
- Describe different types of joints
- Understand how muscles work with bones
- Practice habits for healthy bones and muscles

## Theme 2: Plants - Reproduction and Dispersal
### Content

**Parts of a Flower**
- Sepals: protect the flower bud (usually green)
- Petals: attract insects (colorful, fragrant)
- Stamens: male part (anther produces pollen, filament supports anther)
- Pistil/Carpel: female part (stigma, style, ovary)
- Ovary contains ovules (future seeds)

**Pollination**
- Definition: transfer of pollen from anther to stigma
- Self-pollination: pollen from same flower or same plant
- Cross-pollination: pollen from different plant of same species
- Agents of pollination:
  - Wind: light pollen, no smell or color
  - Insects: colorful petals, sweet smell, nectar
  - Water: aquatic plants
  - Birds: bright colors, more nectar

**Seed Formation**
- After pollination, fertilization occurs
- Ovules develop into seeds
- Ovary develops into fruit
- Fruit protects the seeds

**Parts of a Seed**
- Seed coat: outer protective covering
- Cotyledon: food storage (one in monocots, two in dicots)
- Embryo: baby plant (radicle, plumule)

**Seed Dispersal**
- Seeds must spread away from parent plant
- Methods of dispersal:
  - Wind: light seeds with wings or parachutes (dandelion, maple)
  - Water: light, floating seeds (coconut, lotus)
  - Animals: hooks, spines, eaten fruits (burdock, mango)
  - Explosion: pods burst open (pea, balsam)

**Germination**
- Conditions needed: water, air (oxygen), warmth
- Light is not needed for germination
- Stages: seed absorbs water, coat softens, radicle emerges, shoot grows

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify parts of a flower and their functions
- Explain pollination and its types
- Describe seed formation and seed parts
- List methods of seed dispersal with examples
- Understand conditions for germination

## Theme 3: Animals - Classification and Life Cycles
### Content

**Classification by Habitat**
- Terrestrial (land): lion, elephant, cow, dog
- Aquatic (water): fish, whale, shark, octopus
- Aerial (air): birds, bat, insects
- Amphibians (land and water): frog, toad, salamander
- Arboreal (trees): monkey, squirrel, koala

**Classification by Body Covering**
- Fur/Hair: mammals (dog, cat, bear)
- Feathers: birds (hen, parrot, eagle)
- Scales: fish, reptiles (snake, crocodile)
- Shell: turtle, snail, crab
- Moist skin: frog, toad

**Classification by Food Habits**
- Herbivores: eat plants (cow, rabbit, deer, elephant)
- Carnivores: eat meat (lion, tiger, shark, eagle)
- Omnivores: eat both (bear, crow, pig, human)

**Vertebrates and Invertebrates**
- Vertebrates: animals with backbone (fish, birds, mammals)
- Invertebrates: animals without backbone (insects, worms, snails)

**Life Cycle of Butterfly**
- Egg: laid on leaves
- Larva (Caterpillar): eats leaves, grows
- Pupa (Chrysalis): transformation stage
- Adult Butterfly: emerges, lays eggs

**Life Cycle of Mosquito**
- Egg: laid in stagnant water
- Larva (Wriggler): lives in water
- Pupa (Tumbler): transformation in water
- Adult Mosquito: flies out of water

**Life Cycle of Frog**
- Egg: laid in water (frogspawn)
- Tadpole: lives in water, has tail and gills
- Froglet: develops legs, tail shrinks
- Adult Frog: lives on land and water, lungs develop

**Metamorphosis**
- Complete change in body form during life cycle
- Examples: butterfly, frog, mosquito

### Learning Outcomes
- Classify animals by habitat, body covering, and food habits
- Differentiate between vertebrates and invertebrates
- Describe life cycles of butterfly, mosquito, and frog
- Explain the concept of metamorphosis

## Theme 4: Food and Digestion
### Content

**Nutrients**
- Carbohydrates: provide energy
  - Sources: rice, bread, potato, sugar
- Proteins: help in growth and repair
  - Sources: pulses, milk, eggs, meat, fish
- Fats: provide energy, keep body warm
  - Sources: butter, oil, ghee, nuts
- Vitamins: protect from diseases
  - Sources: fruits, vegetables
- Minerals: for healthy bones, blood, and body functions
  - Sources: green vegetables, fruits, milk
- Water: essential for all body functions

**Balanced Diet**
- Eating different types of food in right amounts
- Include all nutrients
- Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables
- Avoid too much junk food
- Drink enough water

**The Digestive System**
- Mouth: teeth chew food, saliva mixes
- Food pipe (Esophagus): pushes food to stomach
- Stomach: mixes and breaks down food
- Small intestine: absorbs nutrients into blood
- Large intestine: absorbs water, forms waste
- Rectum: stores waste before removal

**Types of Teeth**
- Incisors: front teeth, cut food (8 in adults)
- Canines: pointed teeth, tear food (4 in adults)
- Premolars: crush food (8 in adults)
- Molars: back teeth, grind food (12 in adults)
- Total: 32 permanent teeth

**Caring for Teeth**
- Brush twice daily
- Use proper brushing technique
- Avoid too many sweets
- Visit dentist regularly

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify different nutrients and their sources
- Understand the importance of balanced diet
- Name digestive organs and their functions
- Describe the process of digestion
- Identify types of teeth and their functions

## Theme 5: Matter and Materials
### Content

**States of Matter**
- Solid: has definite shape and volume
  - Examples: ice, wood, stone, metal
- Liquid: has definite volume, takes shape of container
  - Examples: water, milk, oil, juice
- Gas: has no definite shape or volume
  - Examples: air, oxygen, water vapor

**Properties**
- Solids: hard or soft, can be held, do not flow
- Liquids: flow, take shape of container, can be poured
- Gases: fill the container, cannot be seen (most), can be felt

**Changes of State**
- Melting: solid to liquid (ice to water)
- Freezing: liquid to solid (water to ice)
- Evaporation: liquid to gas slowly (puddles drying)
- Boiling: liquid to gas quickly (water boiling)
- Condensation: gas to liquid (water drops on cold glass)

**The Water Cycle**
- Sun heats water in seas, rivers, lakes
- Water evaporates to form water vapor
- Water vapor rises and cools
- Condensation forms clouds
- Rain falls back to Earth
- Cycle repeats

**Reversible and Irreversible Changes**
- Reversible: can go back to original form
  - Examples: melting ice, dissolving salt
- Irreversible: cannot go back to original form
  - Examples: cooking food, burning paper, rusting

**Materials Around Us**
- Natural materials: from nature (wood, cotton, wool, leather)
- Man-made materials: made by humans (plastic, glass, nylon)
- Properties: hard, soft, rough, smooth, transparent, opaque

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify the three states of matter
- Describe properties of solids, liquids, and gases
- Explain changes of state with examples
- Describe the water cycle
- Differentiate reversible and irreversible changes

## Theme 6: Force and Energy
### Content

**What is Force?**
- Force is a push or a pull
- Force can change the way things move
- Force is needed to do work

**Types of Forces**
- Muscular force: force from muscles (pushing, pulling, lifting)
- Gravitational force: Earth pulls objects toward itself
- Frictional force: opposes movement between surfaces
- Magnetic force: force between magnets or magnetic materials

**Effects of Force**
- Start movement of an object
- Stop a moving object
- Change speed of a moving object
- Change direction of a moving object
- Change shape of an object

**Friction**
- Force that opposes motion
- Rough surfaces have more friction
- Smooth surfaces have less friction
- Friction can be useful (walking, brakes)
- Friction can be harmful (wear and tear)

**What is Energy?**
- Energy is the ability to do work
- Energy makes things happen

**Types of Energy**
- Heat energy: from fire, sun, heaters
- Light energy: from sun, bulb, fire
- Sound energy: from musical instruments, voice
- Electrical energy: from batteries, power stations
- Mechanical energy: energy of movement

**Sources of Energy**
- Sun: main source of energy
- Food: gives us energy
- Fuels: coal, oil, gas (non-renewable)
- Wind, water, sunlight (renewable)

**Energy Transformation**
- Energy can change from one form to another
- Examples:
  - Electrical to light (bulb)
  - Electrical to heat (heater)
  - Chemical to mechanical (car engine)
  - Solar to electrical (solar panels)

### Learning Outcomes
- Define force and give examples
- Identify types of forces
- Describe effects of force on objects
- Define energy and identify types
- Explain sources of energy and energy transformation

## Theme 7: Light and Shadow
### Content

**Sources of Light**
- Natural sources: Sun, stars, fire, lightning, fireflies
- Artificial sources: bulb, torch, tube light, candle, LED

**Properties of Light**
- Light travels in straight lines
- Light travels very fast
- Light can be reflected

**Transparent, Translucent, and Opaque**
- Transparent: light passes through completely
  - Examples: clear glass, water, air
  - Objects can be seen clearly through them
- Translucent: some light passes through
  - Examples: frosted glass, thin paper, wax paper
  - Objects appear blurry through them
- Opaque: no light passes through
  - Examples: wood, metal, stone, cardboard
  - Objects cannot be seen through them

**Shadows**
- Shadow forms when light is blocked by an opaque object
- Requirements for shadow: light source, opaque object, screen
- Shadow is always on the opposite side of light source
- Shadow has the same shape as the object

**Shadow Size**
- Shadow size changes with position of light source
- Light source close: larger shadow
- Light source far: smaller shadow
- Object close to screen: sharper shadow

**Reflection**
- Light bouncing off a surface
- Smooth, shiny surfaces reflect light better
- Mirrors reflect light very well
- We see objects because light reflects from them to our eyes

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify natural and artificial sources of light
- Explain that light travels in straight lines
- Classify materials as transparent, translucent, or opaque
- Describe how shadows are formed
- Understand basic reflection of light

## Theme 8: Sound
### Content

**How Sound is Produced**
- Sound is produced by vibrations
- When objects vibrate, they create sound
- Stop the vibration, sound stops
- Examples: drum skin vibrates, guitar string vibrates

**How Sound Travels**
- Sound needs a medium to travel
- Sound travels through: air, water, solids
- Sound cannot travel through vacuum (empty space)
- Sound travels slower than light

**Musical Instruments**
- String instruments: guitar, violin, sitar (vibrating strings)
- Wind instruments: flute, trumpet (vibrating air column)
- Percussion instruments: drum, tabla, cymbals (vibrating surface)

**Characteristics of Sound**
- Loudness: how loud or soft
  - Loud sounds: more energy (drum, thunder)
  - Soft sounds: less energy (whisper, rustling leaves)
- Pitch: how high or low
  - High pitch: fast vibrations (whistle, bird song)
  - Low pitch: slow vibrations (drum, bass)

**Pleasant and Unpleasant Sounds**
- Pleasant sounds: music, birdsong, running water
- Unpleasant sounds (Noise): traffic, machines, loud horns

**Noise Pollution**
- Too much unwanted sound
- Sources: vehicles, factories, loudspeakers, construction
- Effects: hearing damage, headaches, stress, disturbed sleep
- Prevention: use less horns, plant trees, use silencers

### Learning Outcomes
- Explain how sound is produced
- Describe how sound travels
- Identify how different musical instruments produce sound
- Differentiate loud/soft and high/low sounds
- Understand noise pollution and its effects

## Theme 9: Earth and Solar System
### Content

**The Sun**
- Sun is a star
- Sun is the center of our solar system
- Sun gives light and heat
- Sun is very far from Earth
- Do not look directly at the Sun

**Earth's Movements**

**Rotation**
- Earth spins on its axis
- One rotation = 24 hours (one day)
- Causes day and night
- Side facing Sun = day
- Side away from Sun = night

**Revolution**
- Earth moves around the Sun
- One revolution = 365 days (one year)
- Earth's tilted axis causes seasons
- Different parts receive different amounts of sunlight

**The Moon**
- Moon is Earth's natural satellite
- Moon revolves around Earth
- One revolution = about 28 days
- Moon does not produce light, reflects sunlight

**Phases of the Moon**
- New Moon: Moon not visible
- Crescent Moon: small curved shape
- Half Moon (First Quarter): half visible
- Gibbous Moon: more than half visible
- Full Moon: complete circle visible
- Then phases reverse back to new moon

**The Solar System**
- Sun at the center
- 8 Planets in order:
  1. Mercury: smallest, closest to Sun
  2. Venus: hottest, rotates backward
  3. Earth: our home, has life
  4. Mars: red planet
  5. Jupiter: largest planet
  6. Saturn: has beautiful rings
  7. Uranus: rotates on its side
  8. Neptune: farthest, blue color

**Stars and Constellations**
- Stars are far away suns
- Stars look small because they are very far
- Constellations: patterns formed by groups of stars
- Examples: Orion (hunter), Ursa Major (Great Bear), Pole Star

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand the importance of the Sun
- Explain Earth's rotation and revolution
- Describe phases of the Moon
- Name planets in order from the Sun
- Identify what constellations are

## Theme 10: Natural Disasters
### Content

**What are Natural Disasters?**
- Harmful events caused by nature
- Cannot be prevented
- Can prepare and take safety measures
- Examples: earthquakes, floods, cyclones, droughts

**Earthquakes**
- Sudden shaking of Earth's surface
- Caused by movement inside Earth
- Can damage buildings, roads
- Safety: duck, cover, hold; move to open area
- Stay away from tall buildings and trees

**Floods**
- Water covering normally dry land
- Causes: heavy rain, overflowing rivers, broken dams
- Effects: damage to homes, crops, spread of diseases
- Safety: move to higher ground, avoid flood water

**Droughts**
- Long period without rain
- Shortage of water
- Crops fail, animals suffer
- Conservation of water is important
- Store water, use wisely

**Cyclones and Storms**
- Strong winds rotating in circles
- Bring heavy rain
- Can cause floods and damage
- Safety: stay indoors, away from windows
- Listen to weather warnings

**Thunderstorms**
- Storms with lightning and thunder
- Lightning is very dangerous
- Stay indoors during thunderstorms
- Do not stand under trees or near water

**Emergency Preparedness**
- Keep emergency kit: water, food, flashlight, first aid, radio
- Know emergency contacts
- Follow instructions from authorities
- Have an evacuation plan

### Learning Outcomes
- Define natural disasters and give examples
- Describe earthquakes and safety measures
- Explain floods, droughts, and cyclones
- Understand safety during natural disasters
- Know about emergency preparedness

## Theme 11: Pollution and Conservation
### Content

**Types of Pollution**

**Air Pollution**
- Smoke from vehicles, factories
- Burning of garbage, fuels
- Effects: breathing problems, diseases, global warming
- Prevention: use public transport, plant trees

**Water Pollution**
- Chemicals, sewage, garbage in water
- Industrial waste
- Effects: diseases, harm to aquatic life
- Prevention: do not throw garbage in water, treat sewage

**Land (Soil) Pollution**
- Plastic, garbage on land
- Pesticides and chemicals
- Effects: infertile soil, harm to animals
- Prevention: reduce plastic, proper waste disposal

**Noise Pollution**
- Loud sounds from traffic, factories, speakers
- Effects: hearing loss, stress, sleep problems
- Prevention: use silencers, follow noise rules

**Conservation**

**What is Conservation?**
- Protecting and saving natural resources
- Using resources wisely
- Ensuring resources for future generations

**Conserving Water**
- Turn off taps when not in use
- Fix leaky taps and pipes
- Reuse water where possible
- Harvest rainwater

**Conserving Electricity**
- Turn off lights and fans when not needed
- Use energy-efficient appliances
- Use natural light when possible
- Unplug devices when not in use

**The Three Rs**
- Reduce: use less (less plastic, less paper)
- Reuse: use again (old bags, bottles)
- Recycle: make new from old (paper, glass, metal)

**Protecting Environment**
- Plant more trees (afforestation)
- Do not cut trees unnecessarily
- Use public transport
- Say no to plastic bags
- Reduce, reuse, recycle

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify types of pollution and their effects
- Explain causes of different types of pollution
- Understand the importance of conservation
- Practice the three Rs
- List ways to protect the environment

## Theme 12: Simple Machines
### Content

**What are Simple Machines?**
- Devices that make work easier
- Help us push, pull, or lift with less effort
- Six types of simple machines

**Lever**
- A rigid bar that rotates around a fixed point
- Fixed point is called fulcrum
- Makes lifting easier
- Examples: see-saw, scissors, pliers, bottle opener, wheelbarrow

**Inclined Plane**
- A flat, sloping surface
- Makes moving things up and down easier
- Examples: ramps, slides, sloping roads, staircases

**Wedge**
- Two inclined planes joined together
- Used for cutting or splitting
- Examples: knife, axe, nail, chisel, teeth

**Pulley**
- A wheel with a groove for rope
- Changes direction of force
- Makes lifting easier
- Examples: flagpole, well, crane, elevator

**Wheel and Axle**
- A wheel attached to a rod (axle)
- Makes turning easier
- Examples: door knob, steering wheel, screwdriver, bicycle wheel

**Screw**
- An inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder
- Holds things together, lifts
- Examples: screws, bolts, jar lids, light bulb base

**Simple Machines in Daily Life**
- We use simple machines every day
- Often machines are combined
- Complex machines are made of simple machines

### Learning Outcomes
- Define simple machines and their purpose
- Identify the six types of simple machines
- Give examples of each type
- Recognize simple machines in everyday objects
- Understand how simple machines make work easier

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 60 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 40 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Very Short Answer (fill in blanks, true/false) - 15 marks
- Section B: Short Answer (2-3 sentences) - 25 marks
- Section C: Long Answer (diagrams, explanations) - 20 marks

### Practical Activities
- Growing seeds and observing germination
- Observing different types of leaves and flowers
- Making a model of the digestive system
- Simple experiments with shadows
- Observing states of matter changes
- Identifying simple machines at home
- Weather observation chart
- Making a water cycle model

- Encourage observation and questioning
- Use visual aids, charts, and models
- Relate topics to local environment
- Develop scientific vocabulary gradually
- Include field trips and nature walks
- Integrate environmental awareness throughout
