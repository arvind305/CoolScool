# ICSE Grade 5 Science Syllabus
## 2025-2026 Academic Year

**Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) - Official Curriculum**

The ICSE Science syllabus for Grade 5 provides a comprehensive introduction to the physical and natural world. The curriculum covers life sciences (human body, plants, animals), physical sciences (matter, forces, energy, light, sound), and environmental awareness. The approach emphasizes observation, inquiry-based learning, and connecting science to everyday life, preparing students for more advanced science studies.

---

## Theme 1: Human Body - Nervous System and Sense Organs
### Content

**The Nervous System**
- Need for coordination and control in the body
- Brain as the control centre of the body
- Spinal cord connects brain to the body
- Nerves carry messages to and from the brain

**Parts of the Nervous System**
- Brain: controls thinking, memory, and body functions
- Spinal cord: protected by backbone
- Nerves: spread throughout the body

**Sense Organs**
- Five sense organs and their functions
- Eyes: organ of sight
- Ears: organ of hearing
- Nose: organ of smell
- Tongue: organ of taste
- Skin: organ of touch

**Structure of Eye and Ear (Basic)**
- Parts of the eye: cornea, pupil, lens, retina
- Parts of the ear: outer ear, ear canal, eardrum

**Care of Sense Organs**
- Protecting eyes from bright light
- Keeping ears clean and avoiding loud sounds
- Maintaining hygiene for all sense organs

**Simple Reflexes**
- Quick automatic responses
- Examples: blinking, knee jerk

## Theme 2: Cellular Organisation
### Content

**The Cell - Basic Unit of Life**
- All living things are made of cells
- Discovery of cells by Robert Hooke
- Observing cells under a microscope

**Parts of a Cell**
- Cell membrane: outer covering
- Cytoplasm: jelly-like substance
- Nucleus: controls cell activities

**Plant Cell vs Animal Cell**
- Plant cells have cell wall (extra protection)
- Plant cells have chloroplasts (for making food)
- Plant cells have larger vacuoles
- Animal cells have no cell wall or chloroplasts

**Unicellular and Multicellular Organisms**
- Unicellular: made of one cell (amoeba, bacteria, paramecium)
- Multicellular: made of many cells (plants, animals, humans)
- Advantages of being multicellular

## Theme 3: Plants - Photosynthesis and Respiration
### Content

**Photosynthesis - Food Making in Plants**
- Plants make their own food
- Photosynthesis occurs mainly in leaves
- Green colour due to chlorophyll

**Requirements for Photosynthesis**
- Sunlight: provides energy
- Water: absorbed by roots
- Carbon dioxide: taken from air through stomata
- Chlorophyll: green pigment in leaves

**Products of Photosynthesis**
- Glucose: food for the plant
- Oxygen: released into the air
- Simple word equation: Carbon dioxide + Water + Light energy -> Glucose + Oxygen

**Respiration in Plants**
- Plants also breathe (respire)
- Respiration releases energy from food
- Oxygen is used, carbon dioxide is released
- Respiration occurs day and night

**Difference Between Photosynthesis and Respiration**
- Photosynthesis: makes food, needs light, occurs in green parts
- Respiration: releases energy, no light needed, occurs in all parts

**Importance of Plants**
- Produce oxygen for all living things
- Provide food for animals and humans
- Help maintain balance in nature

## Theme 4: Animals - Adaptation and Ecosystems
### Content

**Adaptation**
- Special features that help animals survive
- Physical adaptations (body parts)
- Behavioural adaptations (actions)

**Adaptations in Different Habitats**

**Desert Animals**
- Camel: hump stores fat, can survive without water
- Desert fox: large ears to release body heat
- Snakes: active at night to avoid heat

**Aquatic Animals**
- Fish: gills for breathing, fins for swimming, streamlined body
- Whale and dolphin: mammals that live in water, breathe air
- Webbed feet in ducks

**Polar Animals**
- Polar bear: thick fur, white colour for camouflage
- Penguin: thick layer of fat, huddle for warmth

**Forest Animals**
- Monkeys: long arms for swinging
- Tigers: stripes for camouflage

**Ecosystem**
- Living and non-living things in an area
- Producers: make their own food (plants)
- Consumers: eat other organisms (animals)
- Decomposers: break down dead matter (fungi, bacteria)

**Food Chains**
- Flow of energy from one organism to another
- Example: Grass -> Grasshopper -> Frog -> Snake -> Eagle

**Importance of Biodiversity**
- Variety of life on Earth
- Each organism has a role
- Need to protect all species

## Theme 5: Food and Health
### Content

**Nutrients**
- Carbohydrates: provide energy (rice, bread, potatoes)
- Proteins: for growth and repair (milk, eggs, pulses)
- Fats: store energy (oil, butter, nuts)
- Vitamins: protect from diseases (fruits, vegetables)
- Minerals: for body functions (salt, leafy vegetables)
- Water: essential for all body processes

**Balanced Diet**
- Contains all nutrients in right amounts
- Varies with age, activity, and health
- Importance of eating variety of foods

**Deficiency Diseases**
- Scurvy: lack of Vitamin C
- Rickets: lack of Vitamin D
- Anaemia: lack of iron
- Goitre: lack of iodine
- Night blindness: lack of Vitamin A

**Communicable Diseases**
- Spread from person to person
- Causes: bacteria, viruses, fungi

**Ways Diseases Spread**
- Through air: coughing, sneezing
- Through water: contaminated water
- Through contact: touching infected person
- Through food: contaminated food

**Hygiene and Prevention**
- Wash hands regularly
- Drink clean water
- Keep surroundings clean
- Cover mouth when coughing
- Get vaccinated

**First Aid Basics**
- Minor cuts: clean and bandage
- Burns: cool with water
- Insect bites: wash and apply antiseptic
- When to call for help

## Theme 6: Matter - States and Changes
### Content

**States of Matter**
- Solid: definite shape and volume
- Liquid: definite volume, takes shape of container
- Gas: no definite shape or volume

**Properties of States**
- Solids: particles tightly packed, vibrate in place
- Liquids: particles less closely packed, can flow
- Gases: particles far apart, move freely

**Changes of State**
- Melting: solid to liquid (ice to water)
- Freezing: liquid to solid (water to ice)
- Evaporation: liquid to gas (water to vapour)
- Condensation: gas to liquid (vapour to water)
- Sublimation: solid to gas directly (camphor, dry ice)

**Physical Changes**
- Reversible changes
- No new substance formed
- Examples: melting, cutting, dissolving

**Chemical Changes**
- New substance formed
- Usually not reversible
- Examples: burning, rusting, cooking

**Water Cycle**
- Evaporation: water from oceans, lakes becomes vapour
- Condensation: vapour forms clouds
- Precipitation: water falls as rain, snow
- Collection: water collects in rivers, lakes, oceans

## Theme 7: Acids, Bases, and Salts - Introduction
### Content

**What are Acids?**
- Substances that taste sour
- Found in many foods and substances
- Examples: lemon juice, vinegar, orange juice, tamarind

**What are Bases?**
- Substances that taste bitter and feel soapy
- Examples: soap, baking soda, lime water, toothpaste

**Properties of Acids and Bases**
- Acids: sour taste, react with metals
- Bases: bitter taste, slippery feel

**Indicators**
- Substances that change colour in acids and bases
- Litmus paper: red and blue
- Acid turns blue litmus red
- Base turns red litmus blue
- Natural indicators: turmeric, china rose

**Neutralisation**
- Acid + Base = Salt + Water
- Neutral substances are neither acidic nor basic
- Antacids neutralise excess stomach acid

**Common Salts**
- Table salt (sodium chloride): cooking
- Baking soda: cooking, cleaning
- Washing soda: cleaning

**Uses in Daily Life**
- Acids in food preservation
- Bases in cleaning products
- Salts in food and industry

## Theme 8: Force, Work, and Energy
### Content

**What is Force?**
- A push or a pull
- Can change motion, shape, or direction
- Has magnitude and direction

**Effects of Force**
- Start or stop motion
- Speed up or slow down
- Change direction
- Change shape

**Types of Forces**
- Muscular force: force exerted by muscles
- Gravitational force: pulls objects toward Earth
- Frictional force: opposes motion
- Magnetic force: attraction by magnets

**Contact and Non-Contact Forces**
- Contact: require physical contact (push, pull, friction)
- Non-contact: act from a distance (gravity, magnetism)

**Work**
- Work is done when force moves an object
- Work = Force applied over a distance
- No work if object does not move

**Energy**
- Ability to do work
- Cannot be created or destroyed
- Can be transformed from one form to another

**Forms of Energy**
- Kinetic energy: energy of motion
- Potential energy: stored energy
- Heat energy: from hot objects
- Light energy: from light sources
- Sound energy: from vibrating objects
- Electrical energy: from electric current

**Energy Transformation**
- Examples: bulb (electrical to light and heat)
- Fan (electrical to kinetic)
- Plant (light to chemical)

## Theme 9: Heat and Temperature
### Content

**Heat and Temperature**
- Heat: a form of energy
- Temperature: measure of hotness or coldness
- Heat flows from hot to cold objects

**Measuring Temperature**
- Thermometer measures temperature
- Clinical thermometer: for body temperature
- Laboratory thermometer: for experiments
- Celsius scale: 0 degrees (freezing) to 100 degrees (boiling) of water

**Transfer of Heat**

**Conduction**
- Heat transfer through solids
- Particles pass heat to neighbouring particles
- Metals are good conductors

**Convection**
- Heat transfer through liquids and gases
- Hot fluid rises, cold fluid sinks
- Creates convection currents

**Radiation**
- Heat transfer without a medium
- Heat from the Sun reaches Earth by radiation
- All hot objects radiate heat

**Conductors and Insulators**
- Conductors: allow heat to pass (metals)
- Insulators: do not allow heat to pass (wood, plastic, rubber, wool)
- Uses of insulators in daily life

**Effects of Heat**
- Expansion: materials expand when heated
- Contraction: materials contract when cooled
- Change of state

## Theme 10: Light
### Content

**Sources of Light**
- Luminous objects: produce their own light (Sun, stars, fire, bulb)
- Non-luminous objects: do not produce light (Moon, planets)
- Natural sources: Sun, stars, fireflies
- Artificial sources: bulbs, candles, torches

**Properties of Light**
- Light travels in straight lines (rectilinear propagation)
- Light travels very fast
- Light does not need a medium

**Transparent, Translucent, and Opaque**
- Transparent: light passes through completely (glass, water)
- Translucent: some light passes through (frosted glass, oil paper)
- Opaque: no light passes through (wood, metal, stone)

**Reflection of Light**
- Bouncing of light from a surface
- Smooth surfaces reflect better
- Mirrors use reflection
- Image in a plane mirror: same size, laterally inverted

**Refraction of Light**
- Bending of light when passing from one medium to another
- Pencil in water looks bent
- Pool appears shallower than it is
- Rainbow is due to refraction

**Shadows**
- Formed when opaque object blocks light
- Umbra: dark central part
- Penumbra: lighter outer part
- Shadow size depends on position of light source

**Eclipses**
- Solar eclipse: Moon between Sun and Earth
- Lunar eclipse: Earth between Sun and Moon
- Why eclipses do not occur every month

## Theme 11: Sound
### Content

**What is Sound?**
- Sound is produced by vibrations
- Vibrating objects create sound
- Examples: vibrating strings, drums, vocal cords

**How Sound Travels**
- Sound needs a medium (solid, liquid, or gas)
- Sound cannot travel in vacuum
- Sound travels as waves
- Speed of sound: fastest in solids, slowest in gases

**Properties of Sound**
- Loudness: depends on amplitude of vibration
- Pitch: depends on frequency of vibration
- High frequency = high pitch
- Low frequency = low pitch

**Musical Instruments**
- Stringed instruments: guitar, violin, sitar (vibrating strings)
- Wind instruments: flute, trumpet (vibrating air)
- Percussion instruments: drums, tabla (vibrating membranes)

**Pleasant and Unpleasant Sounds**
- Music: pleasant, regular vibrations
- Noise: unpleasant, irregular vibrations

**Noise Pollution**
- Harmful effects of too much noise
- Causes: traffic, factories, loudspeakers
- Effects: hearing loss, stress, health problems
- Prevention: reduce noise at source, use barriers

## Theme 12: Electricity and Magnetism
### Content

**Electric Current**
- Flow of electricity through a conductor
- Direction of flow: from positive to negative

**Simple Electric Circuit**
- Components: cell/battery, wire, bulb, switch
- Closed circuit: complete path for current
- Open circuit: broken path, no current flows
- Switch controls the circuit

**Conductors and Insulators (Electrical)**
- Conductors: allow electricity to flow (metals, water)
- Insulators: do not allow electricity to flow (rubber, plastic, wood)
- Safety: use insulators to prevent shocks

**Magnets**
- Natural magnets: lodestone (magnetite)
- Artificial magnets: bar magnet, horseshoe magnet, ring magnet

**Properties of Magnets**
- Attract iron, nickel, cobalt
- Have two poles: North and South
- Like poles repel, unlike poles attract
- Directive property: freely hanging magnet points North-South

**Magnetic and Non-Magnetic Materials**
- Magnetic: iron, steel, nickel, cobalt
- Non-magnetic: wood, plastic, glass, copper, aluminium

**Electromagnets**
- Magnet made using electricity
- Made by coiling wire around iron core
- Strength depends on current and number of coils
- Can be switched on and off

**Uses of Magnets and Electromagnets**
- Compass for direction
- Electric bells
- Speakers and headphones
- Cranes in junk yards
- MRI machines

## Theme 13: Solar System and Stars
### Content

**The Sun**
- Centre of our solar system
- A star made of hot gases
- Provides light and heat

**The Planets**
- Eight planets orbit the Sun
- Order: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
- Inner planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars (rocky)
- Outer planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune (gas giants)

**Earth's Movements**

**Rotation**
- Earth spins on its axis
- One rotation = 24 hours (one day)
- Causes day and night

**Revolution**
- Earth orbits the Sun
- One revolution = 365.25 days (one year)
- Earth's axis is tilted

**Seasons**
- Caused by tilt of Earth's axis
- Summer: hemisphere tilted toward Sun
- Winter: hemisphere tilted away from Sun

**The Moon**
- Earth's natural satellite
- Revolves around Earth in about 27 days
- Does not produce its own light

**Phases of the Moon**
- New moon, crescent, quarter, gibbous, full moon
- Changes in visible portion due to Moon's orbit

**Stars and Constellations**
- Stars are distant suns
- Appear as points of light due to great distance
- Constellations: patterns of stars
- Examples: Orion, Ursa Major (Great Bear)

**Other Celestial Bodies**
- Asteroids: rocky objects between Mars and Jupiter
- Comets: icy bodies with tails
- Meteors: shooting stars (burn up in atmosphere)
- Meteorites: meteors that reach Earth

## Theme 14: Environment and Pollution
### Content

**Components of Environment**
- Living (biotic): plants, animals, microorganisms
- Non-living (abiotic): air, water, soil, sunlight

**Natural Resources**
- Air: mixture of gases, oxygen for breathing
- Water: for drinking, agriculture, industry
- Soil: for growing plants
- Minerals: from the Earth

**Air Pollution**
- Causes: vehicles, factories, burning waste
- Pollutants: smoke, carbon dioxide, harmful gases
- Effects: breathing problems, global warming
- Prevention: use public transport, plant trees

**Water Pollution**
- Causes: factory waste, sewage, chemicals, plastic
- Effects: unsafe drinking water, harm to aquatic life
- Prevention: treat waste water, avoid dumping

**Land Pollution**
- Causes: garbage, plastic, chemicals, pesticides
- Effects: infertile soil, harm to organisms
- Prevention: proper waste disposal, reduce plastic use

**Effects of Pollution**
- Health problems in humans and animals
- Damage to ecosystems
- Climate change
- Loss of biodiversity

**Conservation and 3Rs**
- Reduce: use less resources
- Reuse: use items again
- Recycle: convert waste into new products
- Importance of conservation for future generations

**Actions to Protect Environment**
- Plant trees
- Save water and electricity
- Use eco-friendly products
- Spread awareness
- Follow environmental rules

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Grade 5 Science, students should be able to:

- Describe the nervous system and functions of sense organs
- Explain the basic structure of cells and differences between plant and animal cells
- Describe photosynthesis and respiration in plants
- Explain adaptations in animals and food chains in ecosystems
- Identify nutrients and explain the importance of balanced diet
- Classify substances as acids, bases, or salts using indicators
- Differentiate between states of matter and types of changes
- Describe different types of forces and forms of energy
- Explain heat transfer through conduction, convection, and radiation
- Describe properties of light including reflection and refraction
- Explain how sound is produced and travels
- Build simple electric circuits and describe magnetic properties
- Identify planets and explain Earth's movements
- Understand causes and effects of pollution and ways to conserve environment

## Assessment

### Components
- Written Examination: 80 marks
- Internal Assessment / Practical Work: 20 marks
- Total: 100 marks

### Examination Pattern
- Section A: Very Short Answer (MCQs, fill in blanks, true/false) - 20 marks
- Section B: Short Answer (definitions, reasons, differences) - 30 marks
- Section C: Long Answer (diagrams, detailed explanations) - 30 marks

### Practical Activities
- Observing cells under microscope
- Testing for starch in leaves (photosynthesis)
- Testing acids and bases with indicators
- Experiments on states of matter
- Making simple circuits
- Making an electromagnet
- Observing shadows and reflection
- Sorting magnetic and non-magnetic materials
